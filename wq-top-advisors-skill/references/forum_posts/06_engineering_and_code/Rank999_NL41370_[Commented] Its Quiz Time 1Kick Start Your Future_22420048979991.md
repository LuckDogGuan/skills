### 评论 #6 (作者: HY98874, 时间: 2年前)

No,

A knight's tour means a sequence of moves where a knight on a chessboard visits every square exactly once. Imagine a knight moving in an L-shape: two squares in one direction and then one square in a perpendicular direction.

On a standard 8x8 chessboard, this kind of tour is possible. However, if you cut out two squares that are diagonal from each other, the knight can't jump between those two missing squares. This interruption makes it impossible for the knight to visit every square exactly once because it can't complete its tour.

So, in short, removing those two squares messes up the knight's ability to move around the board in a way that visits every square once.
