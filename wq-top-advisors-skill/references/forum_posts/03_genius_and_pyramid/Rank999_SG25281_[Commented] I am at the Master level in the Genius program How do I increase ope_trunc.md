### 评论 #3 (作者: KS69567, 时间: 1年前)

To increase your  **operator count**  while reducing the  **operators per alpha** , you can follow these strategies:

1. **Optimize and Combine Operators** :
   - Use operators that combine multiple actions in one step, such as  `vec_avg` ,  `vec_sum` , or more complex vector operators. This reduces the need for repeating similar operations while increasing the overall operator count.
2. **Use Template Functions** :
   - Create reusable functions that group operators. This allows you to use the same operators across multiple parts of your alpha without repeating them, maximizing your operator usage while maintaining a clean design.
3. **Introduce Advanced Operators** :
   - Gradually incorporate more advanced operators into your strategy. These often perform more complex operations but can count as a single operator, adding variety to your strategy without increasing the number of operators used within each alpha.
4. **Apply Grouping and Time-Series Operations** :
   - Use group-based operators (like  `group_vector_proj` ) or time-series operators that can be applied to broader datasets, increasing the complexity of your alpha without significantly adding to the operator count.
5. **Leverage Conditional Logic** :
   - Use conditions like  `if`  and  `when`  to branch logic in your alpha, allowing you to incorporate multiple pathways or scenarios using a limited number of operators.
6. **Focus on Alpha Consistency** :
   - Ensure that even with fewer operators per alpha, your alpha performs consistently across various market conditions. This enhances your chances of advancing to the next level by improving the overall quality of your submissions.

By focusing on these strategies, you can increase the overall operator count while keeping the operator per alpha low, helping you progress to the next level in your research.
