### 评论 #19 (作者: TL60820, 时间: 2个月前)

Managing the  **color-coding**  of submitted alphas is a common organizational tactic, yet the "empty pool" issue you encountered highlights a critical distinction between  **private metadata**  and  **platform-level visibility** . Most quantitative platforms treat color labels as a local, user-specific tagging system. This means that while you can visually categorize your own "Green" (regular) and "Yellow" (super) alphas, those labels are usually stripped or inaccessible when browsing the broader pool of "not own" or community-submitted alphas to prevent information leakage.

To manage this effectively, you should transition from using color as a primary filter to using  **quantitative performance metrics**  as your first-line selection criteria. Colors are best reserved for internal status tracking—for instance, marking an alpha as "Green" once it passes a specific backtest consistency threshold or "Yellow" when it exhibits low correlation with your existing portfolio.

If you find your filtered pools are empty, it is likely because the metadata schema for external alphas does not support the same labeling fields. Instead of filtering by color, try creating  **Custom Collections**  or folders. This bypasses the limitations of color-coded conditions and allows you to curate a universe of both personal and external alphas that meet your specific risk-adjusted return requirements.
