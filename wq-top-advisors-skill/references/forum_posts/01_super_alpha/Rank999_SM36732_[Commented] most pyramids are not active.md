### 评论 #8 (作者: AC63290, 时间: 1年前)

Regarding pyramids showing as active vs inactive, this is normal behavior and there are several reasons why only some pyramids might be active:

1. Performance Requirements:

- Pyramids must maintain certain performance metrics to remain active
- They need to meet minimum Sharpe ratio thresholds
- Must maintain acceptable correlation levels

1. Common Reasons for Inactive Status:

- Performance degradation over time
- High correlation with existing pyramids
- Failed to meet minimum requirements in recent periods
- System updates or recalibrations affecting performance

1. Best Practices:

- Regularly monitor pyramid performance
- Diversify your pyramid strategies
- Consider creating new pyramids if older ones become inactive
- Focus on quality over quantity

It's normal to have only a portion of your pyramids active at any given time. This helps maintain the overall quality of the platform's alpha pool.

### 评论 #10 (作者: PL15523, 时间: 1年前)

- Here are a few reasons why this might be happening:
  1. **Model Criteria Not Met** : If the pyramids’ criteria (like minimum returns or certain thresholds) are not met, they might show as inactive.
  2. **Insufficient Data** : Sometimes pyramids depend on having a certain amount of data. If the data required for those pyramids is not present or too sparse, they can become inactive.
  3. **Inconsistent or Missing Data** : Missing or inconsistent data can prevent pyramids from being activated. Ensure that your data is properly cleaned and complete.
  4. **Parameter Settings** : If the pyramid's parameters are too restrictive, it may not activate. Consider relaxing some of the conditions (like risk limits or return targets).
