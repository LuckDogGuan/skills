Besides common risk factors like market beta and investment style, liquidity is also a crucial factor to neutralize. An alpha signal may generate high theoretical returns, but if it focuses on illiquid stocks, transaction costs and slippage can significantly erode actual performance. Therefore, controlling liquidity risk such as adjusting portfolio weights based on average trading volume or avoiding low-liquidity stocks is essential to ensure that the alpha can be effectively implemented in practice.
