## 帖子正文

The term  **"return"** generally refers to the profit or loss made on an investment over a certain period of time, and it is often expressed as a percentage of the initial investment. Understanding returns is essential for evaluating the performance of an investment.

**1. Capital Gain (or Loss)** 
Capital Gain: This is the profit made from the increase in the value of an asset (like stocks, bonds, or real estate) when it is sold.
Formula:

**Capital Gain = Selling Price − Purchase Price**

Capital Loss: If the asset is sold for less than it was purchased, it results in a capital loss.

**2. Income Return** 
This refers to the income generated from an investment, such as dividends from stocks or interest from bonds.
Dividend Return: The return that comes from dividends paid by a stock.
Interest Return: The return from interest payments on bonds or other fixed-income investments.
 **3. Total Return** 
Total Return includes both capital gains (or losses) and any income (such as dividends or interest) generated by the investment over a specific period. It provides a fuller measure of an investment’s performance.
Formula:
 **Total Return = {(Ending Value + Income − Initial Value) / Initial Value} × 100**

 Return=Ending Value−Initial ValueInitial Value×100

   - **Total Return**: Includes both capital gains and dividends or interest earned.
