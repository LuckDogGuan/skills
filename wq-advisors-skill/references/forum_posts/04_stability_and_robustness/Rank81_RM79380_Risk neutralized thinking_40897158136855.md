So, what exactly are risk neutralized alphas?🤔

**Risk-neutralized**  alphas are alphas where the platform automatically removes exposure to common risk factors before evaluating performance.

Instead of your alpha accidentally making money from things like:

- market direction,
- industry bias,
- size exposure,
- momentum,
- short-term reversal,
- crowding,

the system tries to isolate the  *unique predictive signal*  your alpha actually contains.

*For example* :

If your alpha performs well only because it constantly longs small-cap tech stocks, then the returns may disappear once size and industry effects are removed. Risk neutralization checks whether your idea still works after those common effects are stripped out.

Think of it like this:

*Your raw alpha return =*  ***true signal + hidden risk exposures***

Risk neutralization attempts to remove the hidden exposures so you see the  **true signal only.**

That is why risk-neutralized alphas are often:

- more robust OS,
- less correlated,
- more portfolio friendly,
- less regime dependent.
