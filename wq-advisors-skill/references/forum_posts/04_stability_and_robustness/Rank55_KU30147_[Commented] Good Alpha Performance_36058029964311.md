### 评论 #8 (作者: TP19085, 时间: 6个月前)

A high-quality alpha is best judged using a balanced set of metrics rather than a single headline number. Measures such as Sharpe ratio, turnover, return consistency, and drawdown together give a clearer picture of true robustness. For instance, an alpha with a Sharpe above 2, reasonable turnover, stable performance across different time windows, and shallow drawdowns suggests a well-rounded and resilient signal. In practice, alphas showing Sharpe above roughly 1.5, turnover under 20%, steady fitness, and controlled downside risk are generally viewed as dependable. When choosing between candidates, it is usually better to prioritize signals that deliver these characteristics consistently across subuniverses instead of those that achieve brief performance spikes in limited periods. Ultimately, long-term stability and sustainability tend to outweigh short-lived peaks, especially in competitive environments where durability matters more than isolated bursts of performance.
