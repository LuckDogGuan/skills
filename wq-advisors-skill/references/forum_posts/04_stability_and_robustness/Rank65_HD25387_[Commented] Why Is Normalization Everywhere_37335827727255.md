## 帖子正文

Is ranking or z-scoring just a standard habit, or is it solving deeper problems?
What happens when one raw input dominates simply because of scale?
Are we normalizing for unit consistency—or for robustness?

---

## 讨论与评论 (2)

### 评论 #1 (作者: KG79468, 时间: 5个月前)

Ranking and z-scoring solve more than unit mismatch. They control dominance from extreme values, stabilize cross-sectional comparisons, and improve robustness. Without them, large-scale inputs often overwhelm genuinely informative signals.

---

### 评论 #2 (作者: 顾问 HD25387 (Rank 65), 时间: 5个月前)

Normalization is doing much more than fixing units. It equalizes influence across inputs, limits outlier dominance, and makes cross-sectional comparisons meaningful. In practice, it’s a robustness tool—without normalization, scale effects often masquerade as signal and lead to fragile alphas.
