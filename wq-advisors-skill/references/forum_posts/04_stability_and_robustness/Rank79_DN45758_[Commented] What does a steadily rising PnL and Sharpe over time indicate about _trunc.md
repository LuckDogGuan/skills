## 帖子正文

In one year, Strategy A's PnL and Sharpe indicate a significant jump, but in subsequent years, its performance is inconsistent.
Despite the small yearly returns, Strategy B exhibits a continuously increasing PnL and Sharpe ratio over a number of year.

Which approach do you think has the best chance of producing a strong alpha, and why is consistent performance important over the long term?

Consistency matters more in the long run. A steadily rising PnL and Sharpe usually indicates robustness and lower risk of overfitting, while one-off spikes may just capture noise or regime-specific effects.
