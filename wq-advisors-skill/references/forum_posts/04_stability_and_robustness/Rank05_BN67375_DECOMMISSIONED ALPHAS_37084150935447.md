### 评论 #11 (作者: CM46415, 时间: 2个月前)

A  **decommissioned alpha**  simply means the alpha has been  **retired from active use or evaluation**  in the platform.

In practice, this happens when:

- The datafield (e.g. something like  *oth455* ) is  **removed, deprecated, or no longer supported**
- The alpha becomes  **invalid under current system rules**
- Or the signal is  **no longer usable in simulations or scoring**

So even if the logic was fine before, once the underlying datafield is changed or discontinued, the alpha can’t run reliably anymore and is therefore decommissioned.

Key point:
It does  **not always mean the idea was bad** —often it just means the  **data source or structure is no longer available or supported** , so the alpha becomes inactive and needs rebuilding with updated fields.
