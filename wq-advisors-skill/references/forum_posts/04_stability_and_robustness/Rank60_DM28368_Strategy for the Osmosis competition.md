## 帖子正文

1. Do we need to participate in the Osmosis program if we are not scoring any alphas?2. Is there a minimum or maximum limit on the number of alphas that can be scored per region? Are there any best practices or recommendations from the admin regarding alpha scoring strategies?

## 讨论与评论 (3)

### 评论 #1 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 6个月前)

2. There is no fixed hard limit on the number of alphas that can be scored per region, but in practice only a limited subset meaningfully contributes due to correlation, quality thresholds, and tie-breaker rules. Submitting many similar alphas does not increase score and can even dilute it. Best practice is to focus on a smaller set of high-quality, low-correlation alphas with strong OS metrics, diversified datasets, and varied neutralizations. Administrators generally encourage quality, diversity, and robustness over volume, since scoring favors unique, stable contributions rather than raw count.

### 评论 #3 (作者: DL51264, 时间: 5个月前)

If you’re not scoring alphas yet, joining Osmosis is still useful just to understand how allocation and ranking work, but the real value comes once you have signals to score. There’s no hard min or max per region, but best practice is quality over quantity. A few strong, low-correlated alphas usually work better than spreading points too thin.
