India’s market characteristics—fewer trading days, lower average liquidity, higher noise, and stronger short-term volatility—make it harder for alphas to maintain consistent Sharpe across sub-universes.

Many strategies that work elsewhere fail here because they’re too reactive, too noisy, or overly dependent on a narrow liquidity bucket. Common “tricks” that help include smoother time-series operators, higher decay, conservative turnover, and stronger neutralization (often sector > mcap).
