### 评论 #3 (作者: FM47631, 时间: 2个月前)

Not even close. A strategy winning 70% of the time but losing 3x on the losers has negative expectancy and will quietly bleed your portfolio dry. Win rate without the payoff ratio is half a sentence. The real question is: what does the average winner look like versus the average loser? A 40% win rate strategy with a 3:1 reward-to-risk ratio will outperform it every time. In quant investing especially, Sharpe, max drawdown, and turnover costs will tell you far more about a strategy's health than how often it is right.

### 评论 #6 (作者: CS51388, 时间: 2个月前)

Not necessarily. A 70% win rate  *sounds*  impressive, but it tells you almost nothing about whether the strategy is actually profitable.

What really matters is the balance between  **wins and losses** :

- If you win 70% of the time but your losses are much larger than your gains, you can still lose money overall.
- Example:
  Win 70% → +$1 each time
  Lose 30% → −$5 each time
  You’ll end up negative despite the high win rate.

### 评论 #8 (作者: ZZ12865, 时间: 2个月前)

A 70% win rate alone does not guarantee a good strategy. If average losses significantly outweigh average gains, the profit factor and expectancy can still be negative. True strategy quality depends on the interplay between win rate and risk-reward ratio. Even a low win rate can be profitable with sufficiently large winning trades. Ultimately, positive expected value and robust risk management matter more than win rate alone.
