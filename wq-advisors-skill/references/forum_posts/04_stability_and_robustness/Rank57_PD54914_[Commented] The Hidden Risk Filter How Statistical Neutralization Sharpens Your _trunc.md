## 帖子正文

**The problem** 
Sometimes an alpha looks strong, but it’s really just riding on known market effects—like size, value, or industry trends. That means it isn’t adding much new insight.

**The solution** 
BRAIN’s new  **Statistical Risk-Neutralized Alphas**  go beyond the usual “fundamental” filters. Instead of just removing obvious risks, they use data techniques like  **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)**  to spot hidden patterns in stock returns and strip them out.

**Why it matters** 
This makes your alphas:

- More robust across different markets
- Less correlated with common factors
- Focused on true, unique signals

**How to try it** 
When running simulations, set  `'neutralization': 'STATISTICAL'` . Test it on your past alphas—you may find cleaner, steadier performance.

**Takeaway** 
By combining statistical and fundamental neutralization, you can design signals that capture real market edges, not just recycled risk factors.
