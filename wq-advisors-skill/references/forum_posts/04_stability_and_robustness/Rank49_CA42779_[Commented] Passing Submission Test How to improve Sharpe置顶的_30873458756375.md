To improve your Sharpe:

1.  Increase Alpha Return

High-quality information leads to better predictions so better returns. You can try:

•   Experiment with different operator combinations and settings, but it will bring you closer to overfitting.

•   The best way is to refine your Alpha ideas with solid economic insights. Idea is the foundation of your Alphas. You can't build a strong house on a weak foundation.

2.  Reduce Volatility

Risks such as specific industry instability or market crashes cause volatility. Proper risk elimination can significantly boost your Sharpe ratio. You can try:

•   Use neutralization settings to reduce exposure to overall market risk or specific groups.

•   Creating a group for group neutralization. You can check it out  [here](https://platform.worldquantbrain.com/learn/documentation/understanding-data/group-data-fields) .

By focusing on these techniques, you can enhance the Sharpe ratio of your Alpha.

Hi, I would like to share some experiences to increase sharpe for beginners. When you first start doing alpha, you should not focus too much on avoiding overfit, because a 'good' alpha will be somewhere between underfit and overfit, and no one can be sure 'how much' is good. In addition, here are some techniques I often use to increase sharpe:
- Use ts_operator and change lookback to find the best parameter.
- Use group_operator and neutralization.
- Focus on exploiting the idea to the maximum and just use simple operators like ts_rank, ts_zscore
- Keep the idea as simple as possible, it will be difficult for beginners to combine >3 datafields in 1 alpha.

### 评论 #22 (作者: CM46415, 时间: 2个月前)

Good summary—this is essentially the core Sharpe trade-off in alpha design.

Sharpe improves when you either increase signal quality (better predictive power, cleaner economic logic) or reduce unwanted noise/volatility through neutralization and risk control. The key challenge is balancing this without overfitting—because excessive tuning can inflate IS Sharpe but hurt OS performance.

In practice, the strongest alphas usually come from  **simple ideas + strong risk filtering** , not complex operator stacking.
