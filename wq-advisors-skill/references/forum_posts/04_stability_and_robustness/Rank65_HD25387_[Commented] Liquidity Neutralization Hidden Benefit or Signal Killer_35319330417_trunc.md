### 评论 #4 (作者: SP39437, 时间: 5个月前)

Liquidity neutralization is truly a double-edged sword in alpha research, and how it affects performance often reveals the real nature of a signal. Liquidity can function either as an unintended structural exposure or as a legitimate source of alpha, depending on the underlying idea. That’s why I usually evaluate both neutralized and non-neutralized versions of the same signal. If performance deteriorates sharply after liquidity neutralization, it often indicates that the alpha was mainly capturing liquidity premia rather than generating independent predictive information. On the other hand, if the signal remains stable—or even improves—after neutralization, it suggests the underlying logic is more robust and likely transferable across universes and market regimes. This comparison is especially helpful when assessing scalability and long-term viability, since liquidity-driven effects can break down as capital increases. Testing both versions provides clarity on whether liquidity is the core edge or simply a confounding factor embedded in the signal.
