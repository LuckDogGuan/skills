### 评论 #2 (作者: GK45125, 时间: 9个月前)

Improving the robustness of an alpha in the China A-share market requires a nuanced approach that addresses its unique characteristics. Unlike more mature Western markets, China's market is heavily influenced by policy, a large retail investor base, and specific trading regulations.

To build a more robust alpha, you should:

- **Integrate Regulatory and Policy Factors** : The Chinese government's role is significant. Use natural language processing (NLP) on official news and policy announcements to create sentiment or event-based signals. This can help your alpha anticipate or adapt to sudden regulatory shifts, like changes in trading rules or government interventions.
- **Account for Retail Investor Behavior** : The dominance of retail investors leads to more herd-like behavior, short-term speculation, and a greater impact from news and sentiment. Therefore, alphas that capture these behavioral anomalies—such as short-term reversals or sentiment-driven factors—may prove more robust.
- **Enhance Data Quality and Scope** : Data availability and quality can be inconsistent. Ensure your data provider offers clean, survivorship-bias-free data. Consider using alternative datasets unique to China, such as supply chain information or satellite data, to gain a unique, less-crowded edge.
