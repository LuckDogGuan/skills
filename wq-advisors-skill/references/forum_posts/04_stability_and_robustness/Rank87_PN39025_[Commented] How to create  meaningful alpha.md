### 评论 #2 (作者: CM46415, 时间: 2个月前)

A strong alpha usually comes from  **clear economic intuition + simple, robust construction**  rather than complex combinations.

Best practices:

- Start with a real idea (momentum, value, earnings revisions, liquidity, sentiment).
- Keep structure simple—fewer operators and inputs reduce overfitting.
- Normalize properly (rank/scale/zscore) to make signals comparable.
- Control risk early: apply neutralization (sector/market) and manage turnover.
- Check correlation with existing alphas to ensure it adds  *new information* .
- Test stability across time (not just high IS, but consistent performance).

A meaningful alpha is usually  **stable, low-overlap, and explainable** , not just high Sharpe in backtests.
