Hello everyone,

Here's a simple method to get started if you're using sentiment data to create Alpha:

Consider the following template: time_series_operator(positive_sentiment − negative_sentiment, days).

What's the concept?
A company's stock price may increase if its general mood improves.

Beginning:

Apply your preferred time series operator to the net sentiment score, such as average, sum, etc.

Time frames that are frequently used include one week (5 days), one month (20 days), one quarter (60 days), and one year (250 days).

Examine the Sentiment Model and try out neutralization methods for a stronger signal.

### 评论 #3 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 10个月前)

This is an excellent and accessible starting point for integrating sentiment data into alpha generation. The template  `time_series_operator(positive_sentiment − negative_sentiment, days)`  captures a core behavioral finance concept—that changes in investor mood can precede price moves. Using different time series operators like  `ts_mean` ,  `ts_sum` , or  `ts_decay_linear`  allows you to target varying signal horizons and sensitivities. It’s worth experimenting with lag structures to avoid overreacting to transient news bursts. Also, applying neutralization against common factors like market beta or sector can help isolate sentiment effects from broader market movements. For more robust signals, you might blend sentiment-derived alphas with complementary fundamental or price-based signals, then test their combined performance and correlation profile. Monitoring stability across multiple time frames and markets will help identify whether the sentiment effect is universal or market-specific.
