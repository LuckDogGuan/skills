## 帖子正文

**Stage** 
 **Status** 
 **Description** 

 **1. Creation** 
Active
You write the code and it passes initial validation.

 **2. Backtest** 
Active
It performs well on historical data (In-Sample).

 **3. Live/OS** 
 **Vulnerable** 
It runs on new, unseen data.  **Most decommissioning happens here.** 

 **4.Production** 
Active
The alpha is robust and accepted into the firm's strategy.

 **5. Decay** 
 **Decommissioned** 
The signal fades over time and is eventually retired.4

### 评论 #13 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 6个月前)

This lifecycle breakdown captures the  *real journey*  of an alpha very accurately, especially the emphasis on Stage 3 being the most fragile. Many researchers underestimate how different  **Live / OS behavior**  is from IS backtests—not just statistically, but structurally. Market microstructure changes, regime shifts, correlation spikes, and crowding effects all tend to surface only once the alpha is exposed to fresh data. In practice, I see Stage 3 as a  **stress-test phase**  rather than a continuation of backtesting. Metrics like rolling IC stability, drawdown shape, and correlation drift become more important than average Sharpe.

I also think it’s useful to view Stage 4 (Production) as  *conditional* , not permanent. Even “accepted” alphas should be monitored for early decay signals—such as rising turnover without return compensation or increasing dependence on specific regimes. Framing decay as a natural and expected stage, rather than a failure, helps encourage healthier research behavior and continuous renewal of the alpha pool.
