### 评论 #2 (作者: KL44463, 时间: 5个月前)

It’s better to value stability across different market regimes rather than chasing the highest Sharpe. Extremely high Sharpe ratios are often regime-specific, fragile, or driven by overfitting, and they tend to break when conditions change. A slightly lower but stable Sharpe across time, regions, and settings usually delivers better out-of-sample performance, survives parameter changes, and integrates more reliably into a portfolio. High Sharpe is attractive, but robust and repeatable Sharpe is what actually compounds over time.

### 评论 #3 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 5个月前)

Stability across regimes is more valuable long-term. High IS Sharpe often comes from overfitting to specific market conditions that don't persist OOS. I focus on consistent performance across rolling windows and different volatility regimes rather than peak Sharpe. An alpha with moderate but stable Sharpe typically survives longer in production. Do you use specific metrics to quantify regime stability, like rolling Sharpe std or drawdown consistency?

### 评论 #4 (作者: TP85668, 时间: 5个月前)

Chasing the highest Sharpe can be misleading. Peak Sharpe often reflects a very specific market regime and may collapse when conditions change. In practice,  **Sharpe stability across time, regimes, universes, and settings**  matters far more than a single extreme value. An alpha with moderate but consistent Sharpe, controlled drawdowns, and low parameter sensitivity is usually much more reliable in production.
