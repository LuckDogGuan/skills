## 帖子正文

**Definition:**

U.S. common stocks (NYSE/AMEX/NASDAQ); exclude zero-dividend names. Rank by trailing cash dividend yield (D/P). Form quintiles each June using NYSE breakpoints; value-weight within buckets.

**What market research finds:**

- Q2 often beats Q1 (highest yield) on risk-adjusted terms because Q1 is crowded with yield traps (distress, cut risk). Several practitioners’ looks at S&P 500 quintiles and recent coverage highlight Q2’s advantage over the highest-yield bucket.
- Dividend growth > raw high yield. Ned Davis/Hartford show long-run strength from  *growers/initiators* ; an investable proxy is S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats (25+ years of raises, equal-weight).

- Shareholder yield (dividends + net buybacks) tends to improve robustness vs. pure D/P screens.

**Minimal Build Notes:**

- Sector-neutral construction
- Basic safety rails (e.g., payout ratio <70%, FCF covers dividends)
- Quarterly or semi-annual rebalance to control churn
- Combine with other signals, using Q2 Dividend as a supporting input
