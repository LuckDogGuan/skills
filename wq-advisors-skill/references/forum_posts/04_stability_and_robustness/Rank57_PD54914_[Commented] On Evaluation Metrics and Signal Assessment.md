## 帖子正文

### *The BRAIN environment provides multiple evaluation metrics — Sharpe, Fitness, Robust Sharpe, OS performance, correlation, turnover, and margin usage. When you’re evaluating your own signals, which of these metrics do you consider most critical? For example, do you prioritize high Sharpe and Fitness, or do you value stability and orthogonality even if it means accepting slightly lower raw performance?*

*How do you interpret situations where an alpha shows great in-sample Sharpe but weak out-of-sample Fitness, or when it performs well in one universe but not another? Do you have any rule of thumb for deciding whether to refine, rebuild, or discard such signals?*
