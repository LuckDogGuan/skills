### 评论 #1 (作者: SY65468, 时间: 1年前)

Good alpha coverage refers to the inclusion of diverse, uncorrelated signals across various datasets—such as price trends, fundamental indicators, sentiment, and macroeconomic variables—to enhance predictive power and portfolio stability. Relying on a broad range of alphas reduces the risk of overfitting to a single data source and makes the strategy more resilient to market regime changes. While single-dataset alphas may offer simplicity, combining multiple alphas leads to better diversification, improved risk-adjusted returns, and more consistent performance. Thus, wider alpha coverage is generally more effective and reliable in building robust quantitative strategies.
