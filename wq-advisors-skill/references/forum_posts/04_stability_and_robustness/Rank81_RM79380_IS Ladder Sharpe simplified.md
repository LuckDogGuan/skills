## 帖子正文

The  **IS Ladder Sharpe test**  checks if an alpha performs consistently over time, with  **more focus on recent performance** .

- The test starts by checking the  **Sharpe ratio of the last 2 years** .
- Then it gradually expands the period to  **3 years, 4 years… up to 10 years** .

For each step there are  **two benchmarks** :

- **Fail Threshold:**  1.58 (for Delay 1). If Sharpe is below this →  **Alpha fails immediately** .
- **Pass Threshold:**  A stricter value (e.g.,  **2.38 for years 2–5** , then slightly lower later). If Sharpe is above this →  **Alpha passes immediately** .

If Sharpe falls  **between the fail and pass thresholds** , the test moves to the  **next iteration with a longer time window** .

**Key Tip:** 
Some alphas worked well in the past but perform poorly recently. Always check  **recent PnL trends**  because the test emphasizes  **recent years** .
