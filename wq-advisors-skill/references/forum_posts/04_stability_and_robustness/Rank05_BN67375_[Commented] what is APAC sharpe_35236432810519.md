## What Is APAC Sharpe?

- **"APAC" = Asia-Pacific**  — includes stock markets in countries like:
  - Japan
  - China
  - India
  - Australia
  - South Korea
  - Singapore
  - Hong Kong
  - and others in the region.
- **Sharpe Ratio**  = a risk-adjusted performance metric:
  ​
- So,  **APAC Sharpe**  is the Sharpe Ratio of your alpha when it is tested on  **APAC stock universes** .

## Why Is It Important?

In WorldQuant BRAIN:

- Your alpha is evaluated on multiple  **regional universes**  (like  **USA** ,  **EMEA** ,  **APAC** , etc.).
- A  **good APAC Sharpe**  means your model can generate  **positive, consistent returns**  on stocks in Asia-Pacific.
- It’s one of the  **“ladder Sharpe” values**  — helping to measure the  **robustness and diversity**  of your alpha across different markets.

## How It’s Used

1. **Alpha Scoring & Ranking**
   Alphas with good Sharpe ratios in  **multiple regions**  (including APAC) are considered more  **robust**  and  **valuable** .
2. **Consultant Promotion Criteria**
   - To become a  **Research Consultant** , sometimes you're required to submit alphas with decent performance in  **diverse universes** , including APAC.
   - APAC Sharpe may be part of the performance thresholds you must meet.
3. **Portfolio Diversity**
   WorldQuant values alphas that:
   - Perform well in  **non-correlated regions**
   - Can be combined into  **diverse portfolios**
   - Are  **unique**  (not just clones of existing models that work only in one region)

## Example

Let’s say you submit an alpha. It might have:

Region
Sharpe

USA
2.1

EMEA
1.6

 **APAC** 
 **0.5** 

Global
1.8

In this case:

- Your  **APAC Sharpe**  is relatively low — the model may not generalize well to Asian markets.
- You might try to  **improve performance**  by:
  - Adjusting features that are  **too US-centric**
  - Avoiding overfitting to US or EMEA patterns
  - Testing more  **macro-insensitive**  ideas

## Summary

- **APAC Sharpe**  = risk-adjusted performance of your alpha in Asia-Pacific markets
- It helps assess:
  - **Regional robustness**
  - **Alpha diversity**
  - **Potential for inclusion in live portfolios**
- A  **good APAC Sharpe**  adds value to your alpha, especially for consultant eligibility and rewards
