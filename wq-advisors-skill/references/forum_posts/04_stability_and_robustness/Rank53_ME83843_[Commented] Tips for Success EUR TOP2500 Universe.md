## 帖子正文

- **Re-test existing TOP1200 Alphas**  on the broader TOP2500 universe.
- **Adapt global (GLB) Alphas**  for use in EUR TOP2500.
- Begin exploring data from  **price-volume, analyst, fundamental, options, and short-interest**  categories.

**Double Neutralization for better stability**

- With more instruments per group, group neutralization becomes more effective.
- Apply:
  - **Country neutralization**  to remove country-specific biases.
  - **Sector/industry neutralization**  for finer risk control.
- You can combine both using  **group cartesian products**  or numeric group encodings.

**Risk-factor neutralization**

- Use group neutralize operators or built-in settings like  **SLOW, FAST, SLOW_AND_FAST, CROWDING,**  or  **Statistical**  risk models.

### 评论 #8 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 5个月前)

This is a very solid and practical checklist for anyone transitioning into or scaling research within the EUR TOP2500 universe. One point that stands out is the suggestion to re-test TOP1200 alphas on TOP2500 rather than starting from scratch. The expanded universe fundamentally changes cross-sectional dynamics: factor breadth increases, group statistics become more stable, and many alphas that were borderline in TOP1200 can either improve due to diversification or fail due to hidden concentration risks. Adapting GLB alphas is also a smart approach, but it’s important to reassess region-specific effects such as country-level macro sensitivity and sector composition differences in Europe. The emphasis on double neutralization is particularly important here—country plus sector/industry neutralization significantly reduces unintended exposures that tend to inflate volatility in larger universes. Combining these via cartesian group construction or numeric encodings gives more granular control. Finally, integrating built-in risk models like SLOW, FAST, or CROWDING helps align alpha behavior with portfolio-level stability, which is essential when dealing with higher instrument counts.
