### 评论 #5 (作者: SC23128, 时间: 9个月前)

In short, yes,  **MAPC points**  do reflect the competence of your alpha, but they are mainly tied to  **in-sample**  validation and submission success. True strength is revealed in the out-of-sample PnL, which is the real test. To maximize your alpha’s competitiveness, ensure turnover remains below 10% and the margin stays above 6% for stronger performance.

Not exactly. In MAPC, the performance points you see are based on how your alpha performs under the competition’s scoring framework, which emphasizes robustness and contribution to the merged pool. They’re not a direct reflection of Out-of-Sample PnL alone. Instead, they combine elements of IS/OS fitness, stability, and incremental value in the merged alpha set.
