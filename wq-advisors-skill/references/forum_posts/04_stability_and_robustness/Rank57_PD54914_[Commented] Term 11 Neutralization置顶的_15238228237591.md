## 帖子正文

Neutralization helps to reduce the risk by making strategies long-short market neutral.
On the BRAIN platform, to neutralize an Alpha, the raw Alpha values are split into groups, and then neutralized within each group - the mean is subtracted from each value.

Imagine you are playing a chess game and your opponent has a bishop that is attacking your king. You might try to neutralize this threat by moving your own bishop to a square that blocks the opponent’s bishop or by trading your bishop for theirs.

Neutralization is a critical step in transforming a raw alpha into a robust, tradable signal. By removing group-level biases, it ensures that performance is driven by true stock-specific insights rather than systematic exposures such as sector or market effects.

The chess analogy captures this well — just as neutralizing an opponent’s attacking piece stabilizes your position, alpha neutralization removes unintended risks, allowing the strategy to focus on its core edge.

In practice, well-neutralized alphas tend to exhibit more stable performance and better generalization across different market conditions.

### 评论 #2 (作者: Douglas Muimi kyalo(DM29981), 时间: 1个月前)

The example of the chess game is similar to how neutralization works in  [WorldQuant BRAIN](https://platform.worldquantbrain.com?utm_source=chatgpt.com) . In chess, a player reduces risk by blocking or offsetting the opponent’s attacking bishop. Likewise, in quantitative investing, neutralization reduces unwanted exposure to certain market factors that may dominate an Alpha’s performance.

On the BRAIN platform, neutralization works by grouping stocks according to categories such as sector, industry, or subindustry. The platform then subtracts the average Alpha value within each group from the individual stock values. This removes the common effect shared by stocks in the same group.

For example, if technology stocks are generally rising because of sector-wide news, an Alpha may incorrectly appear predictive simply because it is heavily exposed to that sector. By applying sector or subindustry neutralization, the Alpha focuses more on the relative differences between companies within the same group rather than broad market or sector movements.

This process helps:

- reduce systematic risk,
- improve diversification,
- create more balanced long-short portfolios,
- and make the Alpha signal more robust and reliable.
