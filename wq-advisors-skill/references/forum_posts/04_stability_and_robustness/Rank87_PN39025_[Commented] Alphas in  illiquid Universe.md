### 评论 #2 (作者: SC73595, 时间: 1年前)

In general, alphas from illiquid universes tend to underperform in the Power Pool competition due to execution challenges and lower signal stability. Their lower turnover and sparse trading opportunities often reduce their competitive edge when compared to alphas optimized for more liquid universes. However, in the broader Pool, some of these alphas can still contribute meaningfully if they exhibit orthogonal behavior or complementarity with existing signals. Careful curation and risk control are key to unlocking their value.

### 评论 #3 (作者: DT49505, 时间: 1年前)

Alphas in illiquid universes present a unique tradeoff between signal strength and practical execution. While it's true, as 顾问 PN39025 (Rank 87) notes, that they may exhibit high production correlation due to reduced noise and fewer high-frequency reversals, SC73595 is right in emphasizing their challenges in Power Pool competitions. Execution slippage, low turnover, and exposure to stale pricing can significantly affect real-world deployability. One potential strategy is to target  **longer holding periods and lower frequency signals**  in these universes, which reduces the dependency on high liquidity. Additionally, incorporating  **volume or dollar-volume filters** , or applying  `group_neutralize`  by industry or sector, may help mitigate concentration risks and improve stability. These alphas may not stand out individually in competitions, but when added to a diversified portfolio, they often enhance orthogonality and improve Sharpe ratios at the pool level.

### 评论 #6 (作者: SP39437, 时间: 1年前)

Alphas developed for illiquid universes face a distinct balance between signal robustness and real-world implementability. As noted by 顾问 PN39025 (Rank 87), these alphas often show high production correlations because the lower noise levels and fewer rapid reversals make their signals cleaner. However, SC73595 correctly points out their limitations in Power Pool contests, where factors like execution slippage, low turnover, and stale pricing pose serious challenges. To address this, targeting longer holding periods and lower-frequency signals can reduce reliance on frequent trades and improve practicality. Moreover, applying volume or dollar-volume filters or using group_neutralize by industry or sector helps manage concentration risk and enhance stability. While such alphas might not shine individually in competitive scoring, they can add valuable diversity and orthogonality within a broader portfolio, ultimately improving the pool’s Sharpe ratio and risk profile.
