### 评论 #3 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 10个月前)

Combining fundamental strength with relative valuation can create a balanced alpha that captures both quality and mispricing. Fundamental strength—measured through metrics like earnings growth, ROE, profit margins, or analyst estimate revisions—helps identify companies with sustainable business performance. Relative valuation—using ratios such as P/E, EV/EBITDA, or price-to-book compared to subindustry peers—ensures that you are not overpaying for that strength. When applied cross-sectionally within subindustries, this approach reduces macro and sector biases, isolating idiosyncratic winners. In practice, you can normalize each factor within the subindustry, apply z-scores or ranks, and combine them via additive or multiplicative methods, possibly with smoothing ( `ts_mean` ) to enhance stability. This blend often improves Sharpe and drawdown characteristics because strength provides durability while valuation offers a margin of safety, and the subindustry framework controls for structural differences across industries.
