# Temporary Update on Daily Simulation Limits — Please Plan Accordingly

## 帖子正文

As discussed in the Opportunity webinar, WorldQuant Brain is introducing a temporary daily simulation limit to encourage high-yield research, ensure optimal system performance, and promote fair usage for all consultants.

📌  **Key Details (Effective from 15th Dec 2025 for one week)**

🔹  **New Daily Limit:**  7,500 simulations per consultant
🔹  **Time Window:**  Rolling 24-hour period (EST time zone)
🔹  **Warning Notification:**  Triggered at 6,500 simulations
🔹  **Temporary Restriction:**  Once 7,500 simulations are reached
🔹  **Access Restored:**  Automatically the next day

⚠️ If the daily limit is exceeded, additional simulations will be blocked until the following day.

⏳ This is a temporary measure aimed at improving overall system performance during the period.

👉 Plan simulations carefully, prioritize high-quality ideas, and avoid unnecessary retries during this window.

### 评论 #1 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 6个月前)

This update makes it clear that the intent behind the temporary simulation cap is not just system protection, but also a push toward  **higher signal-to-noise research behavior** . From a technical workflow perspective, a 7,500 rolling-limit forces consultants to be much more deliberate about experiment design. Instead of large-scale expression sweeps, this period favors approaches like hypothesis-first construction, parameter sensitivity testing, and reusing API settings to stress-test the same idea. It also implicitly rewards workflows that separate  *idea validation*  from  *optimization* , since unnecessary retries and brute-force searches now have a tangible cost.

One practical adjustment is to front-load thinking: validate economic intuition, dataset coverage, and expected risk exposure before simulating. Using small configuration grids (decay, neutralization, universe) to assess robustness can replace hundreds of blind runs. In that sense, the cap acts as a filter—ideas that require excessive simulations to “work” are unlikely to survive OS anyway. Treated correctly, this window can actually improve research efficiency and alpha quality.
