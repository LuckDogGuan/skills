### 评论 #4 (作者: JX93257, 时间: 1个月前)

I think first of all, you want to have a reasonable pool of alphas to select from. Then maybe simply exclude alphas which you think are not good, for example low 2-year Sharpe, low fitness, weak after-cost performance and so on. Some alphas may only barely pass submission criteria, and you may want to totally exclude them.

After that, you can just give equal weight to all remaining alphas as a baseline. Or you can further group them by correlation and give equal weight across groups and within groups, so that highly similar alphas do not dominate the allocation.
