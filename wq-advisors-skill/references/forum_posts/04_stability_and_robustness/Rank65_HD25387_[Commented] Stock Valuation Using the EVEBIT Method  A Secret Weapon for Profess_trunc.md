> ## **What is the EV/EBIT Method?**

EV/EBIT (Enterprise Value / Earnings Before Interest and Taxes) is a stock valuation method widely used worldwide but less common in Vietnam. However, many renowned investors favor this method because it provides a more accurate reflection of a company's true value.

**EV/EBIT Formula:**

EV/EBIT=Enterprise Value (EV) / Earnings Before Interest and Taxes (EBIT)​

🔹  **EV (Enterprise Value)**  = Market Capitalization + Total Debt - Cash
🔹  **EBIT (Earnings Before Interest and Taxes)**  = Pre-tax Profit + Interest Expenses

> ## **When Should You Use the EV/EBIT Method?**

- Compare companies within the same industry
- Identify undervalued stocks for long-term investment
- Suitable for companies with strong and stable cash flows

> ## **How to Apply EV/EBIT in Investing?**

1. **EV/EBIT < 10** : Often considered an attractive valuation and potential investment opportunity.
2. **EV/EBIT > 10** : The company may be overvalued, requiring further analysis.

**Example:**

- Company B has a market capitalization of $500 million, total debt of $200 million, cash of $100 million, and EBIT of $100 million.
- **EV = 500 + 200 - 100 = $600 million**
- **EV/EBIT = 600 / 100 = 6**
- Since this ratio is <10, Company B may be a good investment opportunity.

> ## **Limitations of the EV/EBIT Method**

❌ Does not account for future revenue growth
❌ Not suitable for companies with highly volatile EBIT
❌ Should be combined with other metrics such as P/E, P/S, and EV/EBITDA for a comprehensive valuation

> ## **Conclusion**

The EV/EBIT method is a useful tool that helps investors assess a company’s true value more accurately than traditional valuation methods. However, to make informed investment decisions, it should be used alongside other financial metrics and thorough analysis.
