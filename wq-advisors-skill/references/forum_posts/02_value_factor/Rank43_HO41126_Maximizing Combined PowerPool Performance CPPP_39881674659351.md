## 帖子正文

Combined PowerPool performance is one of the few eligibility criteria that is fully in your control unlike other CAP constraints. That makes it worth prioritizing strategically.

Over the past quarter, I shifted focus more toward Combined performance rather than just improving individual alpha metrics. Here are a few observations that had a noticeable impact

**1. Low correlation matters more than individual strength**

Strong alphas don’t help if they all say the same thing. Submitting simple alphas even if they are weak indivudually but are low-correlated  improved my combined performance significantly.Especially effective during the second month of the quarter also
thematic contests helped generate diverse ideas quickly

**2. Regional diversification is underrated**

Focusing on just one region limits your pool.

What worked better:

- Mixing alphas across global region as well as focusing on other 3-4 regions
- Diversification accross different datasets and datafields

Cross-region diversification naturally reduces correlation and boosts combined Sharpe.

**3. Avoid overusing statistical neutralization**

in time to time people prefer statistical  neutralisation especially in GLB  since it always come with lower correlation as well as good perfoming signals

I noticed better improvements when using RAM and FAST neutralisation while focusing on global region

**4. Out-of-sample (OS) > In-sample (IS)**

A key mindset shift:

Prioritize OS robustness, not IS optimization
Prefer alphas where:

- OS ≈ IS
- or OS > IS

This leads to more stable combined performance.

**5. Simplicity + low turnover wins**

Complex alphas often:

- Overfit IS
- Break in OS

**Final takeaway**

Combined performance improves when you think in terms of portfolio construction, not individual alpha optimization.

- Diversify across regions, datasets, and ideas
- Focus on low correlation + OS stability
- Keep alphas simple and robust
