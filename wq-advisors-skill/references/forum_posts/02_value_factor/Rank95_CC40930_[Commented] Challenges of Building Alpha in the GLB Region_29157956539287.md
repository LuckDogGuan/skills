### **1. Key Challenges**

#### **1.1. Simulation Speed**

- **Explanation:**  The GLB region involves a large and diverse dataset that spans multiple countries, sectors, and asset classes. This increases the complexity and computational demand of simulations.
- **Impact:**  Simulation times for GLB alphas are often significantly slower than for regional alphas, making it harder to iterate quickly and refine alpha ideas. This delay can discourage experimentation and innovation.

#### **1.2. Data Diversity**

- **Explanation:**  The GLB region encompasses a wide range of instruments, each influenced by distinct macroeconomic conditions, market microstructures, and regulatory environments.
- **Impact:**  It becomes challenging to create a single alpha that performs well across such a heterogeneous dataset. The risk of overfitting to specific countries or sectors is heightened.

#### **1.3. Data Availability and Quality**

- **Explanation:**  While the GLB region offers a broad dataset, not all instruments have the same level of data completeness or quality. For example, emerging markets may lack consistent data compared to developed markets.
- **Impact:**  Poor data quality in certain sub-regions can distort alpha signals and reduce the overall performance of the model.

#### **1.4. Cross-Region Correlations**

- **Explanation:**  Instruments in the GLB region are subject to global macroeconomic trends, such as interest rates, trade policies, and geopolitical events. These trends often create high correlations across regions.
- **Impact:**  High correlations make it harder to identify unique, uncorrelated alpha signals, reducing the potential for diversification benefits.

#### **1.5. Execution and Turnover Costs**

- **Explanation:**  Trading in the GLB region often involves higher costs due to currency conversions, cross-border trading fees, and varying liquidity conditions.
- **Impact:**  High turnover alphas may face significant erosion of profitability, particularly in less liquid markets.

### **2. Why Simulation Speed Matters**

Simulation speed is a critical factor in alpha research. Faster simulations allow researchers to:

1. Test and iterate on ideas quickly.
2. Conduct extensive backtests to ensure robustness.
3. Optimize parameters efficiently.

The slower simulation speed in the GLB region can create bottlenecks in the research process, limiting the ability to refine and test multiple alpha variations.

### **3. Strategies to Address Challenges**

#### **3.1. Optimize Computational Resources**

- Use  **parallel processing**  or cloud computing to speed up simulations.
- Leverage efficient coding practices and pre-filter datasets to reduce unnecessary computations.

#### **3.2. Segment the GLB Region**

- Break down the GLB region into smaller sub-regions (e.g., North America, Europe, Asia-Pacific) and build alphas for each segment. This reduces data complexity and speeds up simulations.
- Combine the sub-regional alphas into a broader GLB alpha for final implementation.

#### **3.3. Focus on Robust Signals**

- Prioritize alphas that are less sensitive to data quality issues by using robust datasets with consistent coverage across the GLB region.
- Use  **neutralization techniques**  (e.g., sector, region, or country) to control for global macroeconomic trends and reduce overfitting.

#### **3.4. Incorporate Alternative Data**

- Supplement traditional price and volume data with alternative datasets, such as sentiment data or macroeconomic indicators, to capture unique global signals.

#### **3.5. Optimize Turnover**

- Design alphas with lower turnover to mitigate the impact of high trading costs and slippage in cross-border trades.

### **4. Conclusion**

Building alphas in the GLB region is undoubtedly challenging, particularly due to slower simulation speeds and the complexity of working with a diverse, global dataset. However, these challenges also present opportunities to develop innovative solutions that can outperform in a competitive landscape. By optimizing computational resources, focusing on robust signals, and leveraging segmentation strategies, researchers can overcome these obstacles and unlock the potential of the GLB region. With careful planning and persistence, GLB alphas can deliver meaningful contributions to a well-diversified portfolio.
