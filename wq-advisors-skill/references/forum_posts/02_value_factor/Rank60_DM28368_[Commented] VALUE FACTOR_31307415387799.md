### 评论 #3 (作者: AK40989, 时间: 1年前)

The Value Factor score typically refreshes on a monthly basis, with updates reflecting the performance of submitted alphas from the previous three months. It generally takes around 45 days for the score to be fully updated, so it's important to keep track of your submissions during that period. As for decommissioned alphas, they do not directly affect your Value Factor score; however, they may influence the weight factor, which could indirectly impact your overall performance metrics. Staying informed about the update schedule and monitoring your active alphas will help you better understand how these factors interact.

### 评论 #10 (作者: DT49505, 时间: 1年前)

This thread offers a helpful overview of how the Value Factor (VF) score is updated and what impacts it. To add to the existing explanations, it’s worth emphasizing that the 3-month trailing window used for VF calculations means that consistency across submissions matters just as much as peak performance. A single strong alpha might not move the needle significantly if it's surrounded by weaker submissions. Moreover, although decommissioned alphas don’t directly affect VF, their exclusion from the active alpha pool could reduce your average signal quality if you're not replacing them with better-performing models. Strategically managing your alpha lifecycle—submitting at regular intervals and quickly retiring underperformers—can help maintain a strong VF. Also, monitoring weight factor behavior in conjunction with VF can provide early indicators of whether your alpha design philosophy aligns with what the platform currently values. A deep dive into how weight factor, VF, and alpha retention interact would be a valuable next post.

Great set of questions regarding the Value Factor score. ### 评论 #14 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 1年前)

From my experience, Value Factor (VF) is not just about alpha quality but also  **consistency**  and  **recency**  of submissions. Since VF reflects the  **performance of your last 3 months’ alphas** , one-off excellent models may not shift the score much if they’re surrounded by weaker ones. I recommend maintaining a  **steady pace of diverse, high-IS, low-turnover alphas** , ideally across multiple universes, to create a resilient VF trajectory. Also, while decommissioned alphas don’t directly reduce your VF, they do reduce the size of your active alpha set, which could skew your VF down if not replenished. Monitor both VF and weight factor together—they often trend differently, especially if you're in a transition phase between alpha themes.
