We can't directly view our  **Quantity Factor**  daily from WorldQuant, but here are some tips to estimate it:

- Track your  **overall alpha submission count** , along with  **monthly**  and  **quarterly submissions** .
- Comparing these stats over time can help you gauge your  **Quantity Factor**  trend.
- For  **base daily payments** , the  **Quantity Factor**  is simply the number of alphas you submit on that day.

Consistent submission tracking can give you better insights into your estimated Quantity Factor.

### 评论 #5 (作者: 顾问 JN96079 (Rank 44), 时间: 9个月前)

You cannot know the  **Quantity Factor value,**  like how you see the Value Factor and Weight Factor from the profile section. However, Quantity Factor leads to Weight Factor, in which the value of Weight Factor reflects the amount or ratio of the alphas from your pool being actively used in real market trading. So, the  **Quantity Factor**  is generally determined by the number of alphas you submit or produce, but it is not as important as others, i.e., Weight Factor and Value Factor, because it does not really reflect your performance, only the number/amount of alphas, which includes both poor and good performing alphas if you have both in your pool.

What I can say is that, try to consider mostly the other factors, Value Factor and Weight Factor, since they are the real deal, reflecting your general performance and value to the company at large.
