**Case Study: Universe Expansion & Alpha Quality** 
During the EUR market’s recent universe expansion to TOP2500, I shifted from EURTOP1200 to leverage the broader dataset. This allowed me to uncover numerous high-quality first- and second-order alphas, including a remarkable second-order alpha with fit3.3 and prod 0.43. While first-order alphas used less data, their stability delivered clearer income gains.

Interestingly, my compensation dipped after an initial peak but later rebounded due to  *theme-driven amplification* . However, this recovery occurred despite having no first-order alphas to submit—highlighting how alpha quality can rival the impact of thematic boosts.

### **Critical Mistakes & Strategic Adjustments**

**1. The Pitfall of Quantity Over Quality** 
Early on, I prioritized submitting as many alphas as possible, yet my value factor plateaued at 0.56. While newcomers  *can*  benefit from diversifying submissions,  **low-performing alphas erode overall value** . A health-related hiatus in November forced me to hastily submit 10 mediocre alphas to meet deadlines, which dragged down my initial value factor. Later attempts to compensate with volume only proved unsustainable.

**Lesson:**  In January, I adopted a "quality-first" approach:  *No submissions unless they enhance overall performance.*  This shift emphasized strategic patience over rushed output.

**2. Simulation Efficiency Matters** 
Limited daily simulations reduce both the quantity  *and*  quality of discoverable alphas. To address this, I implemented  **multi-threaded simulations** , significantly boosting efficiency. More simulations mean more high-quality candidates, creating a virtuous cycle of improvement.

### **Dispelling a Common Myth**

Some may regret "wasting" high-quality alphas during low value factor phases. However,  **without early submissions of strong alphas, achieving a high value factor is impossible** . The value factor reflects the platform’s recognition of your alpha quality—low scores signal room for improvement, not a reason to withhold good work.

### **Final Thoughts**

Progress demands balancing experimentation with discipline. Key takeaways:

- Prioritize alpha  **quality**  over quantity.
- Optimize simulation workflows (e.g., multi-threading).
- Submit strategically—every alpha should add value.

This article reflects my personal views, and I welcome your thoughts in the comments! If this resonates, don’t hesitate to like or share—let’s grow together!
