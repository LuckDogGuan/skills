### 评论 #1 (作者: 顾问 JN96079 (Rank 44), 时间: 10个月前)

Great question. the weight factor is brought about by the number or pool of alphas ready to be used in live trading, so the higher your weight factor value, the more your alphas are being actively used. On the other hand, the Value factor describes your value in quality signal production. In general terms, value factors determine the base payment for the daily pay amount and the overall quarterly payout. In contrast, the weight factor only accounts for the quarterly payout. So, they combine both factors at the end of the quarter to determine your quarterly payment.

### 评论 #2 (作者: ML46209, 时间: 10个月前)

Both are important, but they serve different purposes:

- **Value factor**  reflects the  *quality of the signal itself*  (how strong and stable the alpha idea is).
- **Weight factor**  reflects  *how much impact that alpha has in the portfolio*  (scaling with capital allocation).

In practice, a strong value factor is the foundation — without it, weight doesn’t matter because a weak signal scaled up just increases risk. Once you have reliable value factors, optimizing weights can further improve portfolio performance.
