## 帖子正文

Hi everyone, I saw that we don't have many paper for new users, so I want to introduce my favorite paper: "A Five-Factor Asset Pricing Model" by Fama. I read this paper since I built my first WQBrain alpha, about 8 years ago.

Abstract:

A five-factor model directed at capturing the size, value, profitability, and investment patterns in average stock returns performs better than the three-factor model of Fama and French (FF 1993). The five-factor model’s main problem is its failure to capture the low average returns on small stocks whose returns behave like those of firms that invest a lot despite low profitability. The model’s performance is not sensitive to the way its factors are defined. With the addition of profitability and investment factors, the value factor of the FF three-factor model becomes redundant for describing average returns in the sample we examine.

Idea: This paper show how five main factors impact return of stock: Size, value, profitability, investment, and market return. Each factor itself can implement to build an alpha. Such as, value can understand as book value per share or earning per share, so we can have multiple way to brainstorm the idea. The paper also show that Size, value and market return have positive correlation related to returns, while the Profitability and investment having negative correlation, or reversal, which can explain why the high profitibility ratio can lead to reversal idea.

Data can used with this paper: as Users or Consultants, almost data in WQ Brain belong to one of these five factors, here is my classification for some unusual dataset:

- Sentiment/ Institution can be used as investment idea

- Marco/Earning can be used as value idea

- Risk can used as profitability idea

Hope this paper can help you approach and understand how can find idea for your alpha. If you want to discuss anything, please let me know by comments.
