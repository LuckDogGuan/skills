Interesting question — I’d say both luck and strategy play a role, but in different ways.

From my experience:

- Strategy matters for consistency: when I saw a Value Factor increase tied to well-defined logic (e.g., neutralization choices, robustness tests, or blending signals), it usually held up across time and universes.
- Luck matters for timing: sometimes a factor’s jump aligned with market regimes, which later faded. If the design wasn’t grounded, the gains didn’t persist.

So when my Value Factor rises, I ask:  *Did I just catch a regime shift, or does the structure still add explanatory power outside that window?*  That reflection helps separate genuine signal strength from noise.

Curious to hear how others validate their “strategy vs. luck” moments.
