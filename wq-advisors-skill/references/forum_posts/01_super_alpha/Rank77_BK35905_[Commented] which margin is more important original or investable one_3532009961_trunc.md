### 评论 #3 (作者: AC75253, 时间: 8个月前)

That’s a great question and an important distinction to make in alpha evaluation. While the original margin provides a benchmark for raw model efficiency, the investable margin is ultimately more relevant from a deployment standpoint since it reflects performance after liquidity, slippage, and execution constraints. Prioritizing investable margin ensures that the signal translates into real-world profitability rather than just theoretical strength. Using tools like MaxTrade ON, as JC84638 mentioned, can further optimize execution and improve true investability — striking the right balance between alpha potential and practical performance sustainability.

### 评论 #4 (作者: 顾问 BK35905 (Rank 77), 时间: 8个月前)

Yes, the investable (after-cost) margin should generally take priority, since it reflects real-world tradability after accounting for transaction costs and liquidity constraints. A high original margin with poor investability can be misleading, as those gains may vanish once costs are applied. Sorting by after-cost (investable) margin gives a truer picture of sustainable performance.
