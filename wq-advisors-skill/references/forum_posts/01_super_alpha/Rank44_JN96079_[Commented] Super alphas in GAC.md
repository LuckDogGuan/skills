### 评论 #11 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 9个月前)

This is a very good clarification question because the rules of the Global Alpha Competition (GAC) are sometimes confusing, especially when different alpha types like Super Alphas or Power Pool Alphas come into play. Based on the guidelines and community experience, only  *regular*  alphas are officially eligible for GAC scoring. Super Alphas are essentially weighted blends or optimized combinations, and the competition framework is designed to evaluate the robustness of single, standalone signals. This avoids the risk of “meta-optimization” overshadowing the raw research quality of individual alphas. Power Pool alphas can occasionally appear in scoring if they meet the same robustness checks as regular alphas (Sharpe, fitness, OS/IS balance, etc.), but that seems more like an overlap in categorization rather than a deliberate inclusion. The important takeaway is that if you want consistent GAC consideration, it’s best to focus on strong, regular alpha submissions that meet the platform’s eligibility criteria.
