## 帖子正文

Turnover rate—the frequency of buying and selling assets—is a key factor in designing successful alpha strategies. The right balance between turnover and alpha generation depends on market conditions, transaction costs, and risk tolerance.
How do you practically assess market conditions to choose your turnover rate?

### 评论 #4 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 9个月前)

A practical way to set turnover is by stress-testing alphas across different regimes. In high-volatility, liquid markets, higher turnover can capture fast signals, while in calmer or costlier environments, lower turnover preserves returns after costs. Many quants track liquidity measures, bid-ask spreads, and volatility levels to adjust turnover expectations dynamically. Backtesting with realistic transaction costs and slippage is essential—if an alpha only works at unrealistically high turnover, it’s likely fragile.
