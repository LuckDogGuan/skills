## 帖子正文

Throughout my journey, I've engaged with approximately a dozen datasets. The process has been a mix of long durations and varying outcomes. Some datasets required several days of processing, only to yield no alphas by the end of the third stage. In contrast, others were much more fruitful, with hundreds of alphas ready for detection and submission by the second stage.

From these experiences, I've learned that if, after over 2000 simulations in the first stage, no alpha with a Sharpe ratio greater than 1 emerges, it's a clear signal to switch datasets. This insight is backed by the collective wisdom and practical findings of those who have tread this path before me. The ASI region, in particular, stands out as a fertile ground for alpha generation, which aligns with my own successes. One of my datasets from this region produced dozens of alphas that made it to the final submission phase.

When selecting datasets now, my criteria have evolved. I first check if the dataset has a history of producing alphas. If it has, and if the number is moderate with an average value score, and provided that the dataset isn't too sparse in terms of data fields, I proceed to run the third stage.

This approach has not only optimized my workflow but also deepened my understanding of what makes a dataset promising. It's a blend of historical performance, quality metrics, and the richness of data that guides my choices now, steering me towards more productive and efficient data exploration endeavors.
