### 评论 #9 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 5个月前)

This is a very accurate summary of what really matters in Osmosis-style evaluation on  **WorldQuant Brain** . The key technical challenge is that point allocation is effectively a  **proxy for marginal contribution to a diversified portfolio** , not a reward for isolated performance. Giving higher scores to your “best” alphas should therefore be interpreted as assigning points to signals that are not only strong, but also  *stable, low-cost, and structurally different*  from one another. In practice, this means prioritizing alphas with consistent IC across subperiods, reasonable turnover, and low correlation to your own pool, even if their headline Sharpe is not the absolute highest.

Using different datasets and ideas is also critical because redundancy is one of the biggest silent killers in combo performance. Two alphas with different formulas but the same economic driver will often compete for the same returns once combined. Scoring forces you to make that trade-off explicit. A clean Osmosis submission usually reflects a portfolio mindset: moderate allocations across multiple robust themes, capped exposure to any single failure mode, and deliberate avoidance of fragile or over-optimized signals. Approached this way, Osmosis becomes a test of research discipline rather than luck.
