On February 4, 2025, WorldQuant updated its Genius Leaderboard to reflect after-cost combined alpha performance for all alphas submitted until December 31, 2024. Previously, the leaderboard rankings were based solely on pre-cost alpha performance. This shift has introduced a significant change in how user performance is evaluated, directly tying alpha performance to real-world cost considerations.

Key  **Observations**

1. **Dominance of Negative Impact:**
   - The majority of users (66%) experienced a negative impact. This highlights the critical importance of considering cost when constructing alphas.
2. **Minimal No-Change Impact:**
   - Only 19.7% of users maintained stable performance, showing that the new metric has significantly reshuffled the leaderboard for most participants.
3. **Gold Level Users:**
   - As the largest user group, Gold Level participants experienced the most significant shifts, with both the highest count of negatively and positively impacted users.
4. **Strategic Insight for Users:**
   - The update underscores the necessity of optimizing alphas for cost efficiency. Users relying heavily on strategies with high transaction costs or slippage are disproportionately affected.

### **Conclusion**

The inclusion of after-cost combined alpha performance marks a pivotal evolution in Genius Leaderboard evaluations. While it rewards users with cost-efficient strategies, it penalizes those whose alphas incur high trading costs. To remain competitive, users must prioritize the development of alphas that balance profitability and cost-effectiveness.

This shift serves as a wake-up call to all contributors, emphasizing that long-term success in alpha generation requires adapting to real-world trading constraints.
