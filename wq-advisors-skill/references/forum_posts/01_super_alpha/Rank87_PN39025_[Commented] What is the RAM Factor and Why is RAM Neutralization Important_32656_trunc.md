# What is the RAM Factor and Why is RAM Neutralization Important?

## 帖子正文

**Reversion and Momentum (RAM) Factor**  is a combo of two effects in stock prices:

1. **Reversion Factor**  – Stocks that recently fell may bounce back soon. Stocks that rose quickly might drop. It’s like prices correcting themselves over a few days.
2. **Momentum Factor**  – Stocks going up keep going up, and those falling keep falling. It’s a trend that lasts longer.

Neutralizing RAM means changing your plan a bit to avoid losses from sudden ups and downs in stock prices. It helps to:

- **Avoid risky trend-based trades**  that might reverse suddenly.
- **Protect against quick market swings**  that confuse long-term strategies.
- **Focus on real value**  by removing the effect of short-term price moves.
- **Improve overall performance**  by lowering risk and increasing stable returns.
