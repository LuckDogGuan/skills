**Introducing Nong Nguyen Tuan Minh from Vietnam - studying Information Security at the Academy Of Cryptography Techniques**

3rd place in SuperAlpha Competition - Month 3

In his own words!

**How did you enjoy the competition?**

The competition was a thrilling experience. The constant chase and pressure, especially during the IS scoring period, made it a highly engaging and stimulating challenge. The Kenyan consultant consistently pushed me to perform better, adding to the intensity. When the OS scores were announced, the unexpected results made it even more exhilarating.

**Tell us about your journey on BRAIN /What do you want to get out of the BRAIN Consultancy Program?**

I discovered WorldQuant through social media advertisements. Currently, I've been working as a Consultant for nearly two years, during which I've enhanced my creativity and financial thinking.

**What do you like most about the BRAIN platform?**

What I like most about the BRAIN platform is its user-friendly interface. It makes it easy to apply research ideas and learn quickly, allowing users to focus on developing and testing strategies efficiently.

**How do you come up with ideas for new Alphas?**

When I need new alpha ideas, I read research papers and reverse engineer their proposals. This deepens my understanding and stimulates creativity, leading to effective and innovative ideas. By applying this contrarian thinking, I've achieved a value factor of 0.9 for over a quarter.

**Introducing Abhishek Chaudhary from India - Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur, final year studying Chemical Engineering and has experience in machine learning**

8th place in SuperAlpha Competition - Month 3

In his own words!

**How did you feel about coming top of the leaderboard in SAC?**

I am extremely pleased with the conclusion of my month-long hard work. The IS leaderboard kept me motivated to keep working on and submitting better alphas throughout the competition.

**How did you get interested in quant finance**

This goes back to my project on Augmented Financial Intelligence at University College London (UCL). This was a project I was involved in that combined human judgement with machine learning in order understand the price movements of cryptocurrencies. Studying financial time series data and working to develop algorithms got me more interested in how this could apply to finance.  Moreover, my research paper "Augmented Machine Intelligence with Human Intelligence for Cryptocurrency Price Prediction" was published in International Journal of Machine Learning!

**Tell us about your BRAIN journey**

I learned about BRAIN through a friend. I applied myself to the learning and managed to receive an invitation to join the Consultant program and I really enjoy all of the competitions.

**How do you come up with your Alpha ideas**

I try to read research papers in the field of quantitative finance for new ideas. Also, I try to explore new datafields given on BRAIN and add my own logic to these.
