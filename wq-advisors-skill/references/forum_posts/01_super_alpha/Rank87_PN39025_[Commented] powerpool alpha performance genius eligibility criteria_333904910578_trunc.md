### 评论 #3 (作者: SK13215, 时间: 11个月前)

The  **PowerPool alphas don't have separate, lower thresholds** ; instead, they contribute to your  **overall performance score**  just like regular alphas do. So:

- To reach Grandmaster, you still need the same combined alpha performance score required by the official criteria.
- There isn't a  *reduced*  threshold specifically for PowerPool alphas—you can't earn Grandmaster status by only submitting PowerPool alphas unless you meet the full combined score.
- **PowerPool alphas count** , but the target performance goal remains the same.

### 评论 #5 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 9个月前)

The criteria for reaching higher Genius levels are based on  **Combined Alpha Performance (CAP)**  and  **Combined Selected Alpha Performance (CSAP)** , but the thresholds apply to your overall portfolio of submitted alphas rather than to PowerPool alphas specifically. PowerPool alphas do contribute to these metrics, since they are part of your submitted alpha set, but there isn’t a separate or “easier” requirement for them. In other words, you don’t need a PowerPool Alpha Performance of exactly 2 to hit Grandmaster—what matters is whether your total CAP and CSAP across all alphas (regular and PowerPool) meet the thresholds. Many contributors use PowerPool alphas strategically because they tend to improve diversity and correlation structure, which helps raise CSAP and stabilize CAP.
