# Universe Choice Is an Alpha, Not a Setting

## 帖子正文

Most contributors treat universe selection as a background choice.
That’s a mistake.

The universe determines:

- liquidity regime
- factor exposure
- crowding intensity
- signal half-life

Two identical formulas in different universes are  **different alphas** .

If your signal only works in one carefully chosen universe, that’s not cheating — that  *is*  the edge.

The real mistake is pretending the universe doesn’t matter.

**Debate question:** 
Is exploiting universe-specific effects legitimate alpha, or just hidden overfitting?

## 讨论与评论 (2)

### 评论 #1 (作者: KP26017, 时间: 3个月前)

**The condition:**

Universe-specific alpha is legitimate when the universe selection is motivated by an economic hypothesis about why that specific market structure creates the inefficiency you're capturing. It's hidden overfitting when the universe was selected because it happened to make the backtest look good.

That distinction sounds simple but it's doing enormous work. The entire difference between legitimate edge and overfitting in this context comes down to whether the universe choice was a research input or a research output.
