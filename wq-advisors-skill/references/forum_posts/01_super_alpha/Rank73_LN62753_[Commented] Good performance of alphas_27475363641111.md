### 评论 #5 (作者: NH84459, 时间: 1年前)

- **Sharpe Ratio**  is a fundamental measure of risk-adjusted return, calculated by dividing the excess return (over a risk-free rate) by the standard deviation of the returns (volatility). A consistent  **Sharpe ratio**  indicates that the strategy is generating returns per unit of risk in a stable manner.
- **Action** : Monitor the Sharpe ratio over various periods (e.g., 1-year, 2-year, 5-year) to ensure that it does not fluctuate dramatically. A significant drop in Sharpe could indicate a shift in the strategy’s performance that requires adjustment.
