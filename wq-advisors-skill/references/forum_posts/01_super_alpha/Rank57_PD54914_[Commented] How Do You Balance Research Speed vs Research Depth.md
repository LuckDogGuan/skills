### 评论 #6 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 5个月前)

Balancing research speed and depth is really about structuring the workflow so that depth is  *earned*  rather than applied uniformly. In my process, I treat early-stage alpha generation as a broad exploration phase where speed matters more than precision. At this stage, I focus on simple expressions, minimal parameters, and quick diagnostics such as IS/OS Sharpe, turnover, decay sensitivity, and basic robustness checks across delays or nearby universes. Alphas that fail these fast filters are discarded quickly. Only when a signal shows structural consistency — for example, stable IC behavior, reasonable turnover, and no obvious fragility — do I slow down and invest time in deeper validation like cross-universe robustness, crowding exposure, correlation analysis, and sensitivity to parameter changes. This tiered approach prevents over-investing in weak ideas while still allowing strong candidates to be thoroughly refined for OS durability, which is especially important in competitive settings like AIAC.
