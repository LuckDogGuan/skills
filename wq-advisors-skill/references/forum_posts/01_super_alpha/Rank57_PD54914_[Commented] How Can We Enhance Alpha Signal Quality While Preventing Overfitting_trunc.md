## 帖子正文

In quantitative research, optimizing signal quality often comes with the risk of overfitting—where an alpha captures noise rather than actual market structure. Achieving a balance between these two goals is central to building models that are both potent and resilient in live-trading environments.

Some approaches involved in obtaining such a balance are:

Feature robustness testing – feature design and feature selection that still show predictive capability across varying universes, time periods, and regimes of the market.

Regularized model construction – the use of L1/L2 penalties or other constraining methodologies to prevent over-reliance on features and to generalize.

Cross-validation and decay analysis – testing of whether alpha's performance generalizes across rolling windows or new datasets to make sure signal quality enhancement is not a product of data-mining bias.
