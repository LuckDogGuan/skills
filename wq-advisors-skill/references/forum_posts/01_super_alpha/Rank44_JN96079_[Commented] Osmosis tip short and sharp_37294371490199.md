## 帖子正文

> **Concentrate, don’t equalize.**
> Put  **60–70% of your points on your top 2–3 alphas**  that are  **uncorrelated with each other** , have  **stable Sharpe across regions** , and  **low drawdown** . Spread the remaining points lightly to reach the 100k requirement.

**Why it works:**  Osmosis rewards  **conviction + diversification** , not democracy. One strong alpha diluted is worse than two strong ones amplified.

### 评论 #22 (作者: MJ38971, 时间: 5个月前)

hi  HT71201

To validate the top 2–3 alphas, I usually look at  **both correlation and stability** , not just headline Sharpe:

- **Correlation checks**
  I keep pairwise correlations low across:
  - returns
  - drawdowns
  Typically aiming for < ~0.3 over long windows, and I sanity-check that correlations don’t spike during stress periods.
- **Rolling stability metrics**
  I rely more on  **rolling Sharpe / rolling returns**  (e.g., 6–12 month windows) rather than full-period Sharpe, to catch regime fragility early. An alpha that oscillates wildly in rolling metrics is a red flag for heavy concentration.
- **Cross-region consistency**
  Not identical performance, but  *directional robustness* . If an alpha only works in one region or collapses elsewhere, it gets capped in allocation.
- **Structural soundness**
  I’m more comfortable concentrating when the alpha logic is explainable and economically intuitive, not just statistically lucky.

If an alpha is clearly regime-dependent, I agree broader diversification is safer. The 60–70% guideline is really for cases where the signals are  **low-correlated, structurally different, and stable under rolling diagnostics** , not a blanket rule.
