# 🗺 6 Survival Rules:

# 

Hello soldiers, I’m also on the journey of balancing efficiency with serious research, and I’d like to share  **a few hard-earned lessons** . Much of this knowledge comes from my own exploration since the beginning of the year, as well as insights from the  **community**  and  **fellow researchers** . If you’re new to the platform (within 3–6 months), these points can save you a lot of frustration and give you the best shot at climbing toward Master and even GrandMaster levels.  ▄︻デ══━一

### 1️⃣ Build Your Research Engine ⛶

Consult your community, you can also ask AI how to build an MIT-Level research process... it’ll customize the answer for you.

👉 Developing statistical arbitrage strategies will sometimes feel boring and repetitive, and other times creative and breakthrough. That’s the true essence of alpha research.

### 2️⃣ Don’t Overload on Fundamentals ⛏

Fundamental data is very powerful—no doubt about it. But please don’t let your Fundamental Alphas take up more than  **30% of your submissions in any Region** . In fact, the upper limit calculation is more complex, but to put it simply, keeping it below 30% is definitely safe. If you cross that line, you may end up getting locked out for a very long time.

The truth is, fundamentals can be a strong driver of signals, but the system wants you to be balanced. Overweighting them may backfire. I can also tell you that, in general, fnd, pv, and opt are reliable sources of signals.

### 3️⃣ Power Pool = Golden Window ✈

The moment you submit your first Power Pool Alpha (PPA), you unlock a  **3-month unlimited submission period** . Use it wisely—this is your best chance to break into the higher ranks. Think of it as your early “fast lane” to Master or even GrandMaster. Furthermore, if you can’t achieve good results in competitions, prioritize the Genius Program instead. I suggest you take competitions seriously only after you’ve been a consultant for at least three months.

💡 One more thing: based on my own testing, the strongest combination is a  **mix of Regular Alphas + Power Pool Alphas** . Together, they create a balance of stability and punch that’s hard to beat.

### 4️⃣  Value Factor > Quantity  **ⓘ**

Forget brute force.  **Quality beats quantity** .
Your Value Factor (VF) is calculated over 3 months of Alphas, and for newcomers, VF is tightly correlated with Combined Performance.

And later, when you unlock Super Alpha (SA), never get careless—if you pick a bunch of trash signals, they will drag down your quality instead of boosting it. (there’s still plenty left for you to dig into — things like Combo Algo, IC, IR, etc.)

**To give you perspective:**

- I once submitted only ~40 Regular Alphas (no SAs) in two months and reached VF = 0.97.
- Later, I submitted 140~150 in a month and ended up with results ranging from 0.99 down to 0.90.

High VF means bigger  **Daily Pay** , and trust me—that extra motivation is game-changing.

Some may focus on Weight to pursue higher quarterly pay. But from what I’ve seen, beginners should prioritize Value Factor (VF) instead. Weight will naturally increase faster than you think — it goes up every day, and  **if your ProdCorr/SelfCorr is low and your VF is high** , the real difference only becomes clear after around 4-5 months. Also, if you haven’t submitted more than 20 Alphas, your VF  **will not be updated** .

From my observations, when beginners overemphasize creating “differentiated” Alphas just to accumulate Weight, they often end up with a very low VF — because the Alphas they invent are usually flawed.

👉 But don’t panic if your VF fluctuates. That’s completely normal. VF includes a  **self-growth factor** , and your overall portfolio versus your rolling 3-month portfolio will always have differences.

The platform collects a large number of trading strategies to combine (e.g., Pyramids), so it values quantity. But as an individual, you must pursue quality — the two needs are different. Without being told this, you’ll naturally feel inclined to submit more.

👉 The difference in Base Pay between VF 0.99 and VF 0.50 can reach up to  **20 times** .

### 5️⃣ Learn From the Best  🏳

Don’t reinvent the wheel just to be “different.” Study the templates and examples shared in strong forum posts. They exist for a reason. Copy their structure, learn from them, and—very importantly— **leave a like ♡**   **to encourage authors**  to share more.

Also, keep a close eye on the tie-breaker performance across different countries and regions. Try to push yours as high as possible, because sometimes unexpected events can shift the rankings in ways you don’t anticipate.

### 6️⃣ The Target Function Is Everything  ⚔

The secret to sustainable performance isn’t spamming random Alphas—it’s knowing  **what to submit and what not to submit** .

A lot of people misunderstand how recent performance, costs, and strategy combinations actually work. That’s why you see many stuck with VF 0~0.4 and Combined < 0.5.

You need to remember that Alphas losing money after costs will  **most**   **likely keep bleeding**  once they reach OS.

**✦ Here’s the truth:** 
With just a few dozen well-selected Alphas, you can achieve  **Combined after-cost OS > 3** . Although the current Master requirement is only ≥ 1, if you’re below 1.5, I’ll be blunt: the outcome is simply poor. Good combinations can hold strong through every OS update—that’s what real quant finance looks like. On the flip side, plenty of people have watched their OS collapse from 2 all the way down to below 0.5 after updates.

🔥 Bottom line:  **Be selective, be smart, and don’t chase volume.**  If you focus on VF, take advantage of the  *▸▸▸ **Power Pool window ▸▸▸*** , and learn from the forum’s best templates, you’ll accelerate much faster than those who try to brute-force their way up.

If you play aggressively, of course you can  **jump straight from Gold to GrandMaster** — a true quantum leap, a rise from the depths to the sky. I’m a real-life example of this, and fortunately it’s not that difficult, since Gold now gives you access to over 80 Pyramids across USA, GLB, EUR, ASI, and CHN. That said, I strongly recommend filling up USA, GLB, EUR, and ASI first, and leaving CHN for later exploration.

In fact, the calculation of Tie-Breakers is quite nuanced. It follows a strong law of diminishing returns — in certain ranges, even a small improvement in a given metric can earn you a lot of points, but once you go beyond (or fall below) that range, no matter how much effort you put in, the impact becomes minimal.

BTW, since GLB and ASI have new restrictions, EUR is the easiest region for submitting PPAs. As for D0, you can still submit PPAs there, but you need to pay even closer attention to costs compared to D1. Pyramids should be filled strategically — you need to balance both quality and tie-breakers. And if there’s a Theme campaign offering a 2x multiplier, make sure to grab it, since it has  **a huge impact on Base Pay** . I’ve been dynamically adjusting all of this to maximize the overall benefit. (Jing-Ze C)
