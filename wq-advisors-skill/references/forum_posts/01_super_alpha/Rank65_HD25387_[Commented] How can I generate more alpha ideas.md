### 评论 #1 (作者: NT63388, 时间: 1年前)

I think there are two main methods:

1.  **Generating ideas from articles**  - especially those curated by Brain and sent as notifications on the platform.
When you read these articles, you can find ideas and keywords. You can then go to the "Data" section on the platform to search by keyword and implement these ideas.
The Alphas found this way often perform quite well.
However, such ideas are not abundant.

**2. Mining with templates** 
You can use your existing templates or those shared by the community. Apply them by substituting Data Frames (DF) with new Datasets you’re interested in.
Alternatively, a simpler approach is to review your previously submitted Alphas, then try converting similar Alphas into new templates you don’t yet have.

### 评论 #2 (作者: CH62432, 时间: 1年前)

Platform learning resources interact with the community

"Learn" module: The official learning module of BRAIN platform, which systematically explains basic and advanced knowledge such as factor construction and backtest Settings.

Community Forum: An active community forum allows you to see ideas and feedback shared by other researchers and gain insight into real exploration paths.

101 Formulaic Alphas documentation

The collection of classic factor formulas from WorldQuant, which shows how to build different alphas combining price, volume, technical indicators, etc., with an average holding period of 0.6 to 6.4 days, is an important reference for finding inspiration.

Idea of data scenario combination

Use a mix of different types of data sets such as price and volume, earnings, analyst expectations, news, and risk data to build a unique perspective.

The "trade_when" operator is used to construct an event-driven factor to reduce the turnover rate and enhance the signal specificity.

Automated API Exploration

We use the Brain API for batch backtesting and simulation of various parameter combinations (e.g., Decay, Neutralization Settings) to greatly improve iteration efficiency.

### 评论 #4 (作者: 顾问 HD25387 (Rank 65), 时间: 1年前)

One thing that’s helped me is  **building a personal “alpha log”**  where I jot down quick ideas, keywords, or paper references as they come up — even if I don’t use them right away. I also like exploring new datasets directly on the platform and just  **asking: what is this data uniquely good at measuring?**  That usually leads to fresh angles. Reusing existing alpha templates with new data sources is another great way to scale idea generation while keeping structure intact. Finally, reading academic abstracts or Quant Twitter threads often sparks non-obvious concepts that I can test in a simplified form. Idea flow gets easier when you systematize it.
