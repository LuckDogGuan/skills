## 帖子正文

I am planning to adopt the following strategy for OSMOSIS:

1) Diversification is the key: I would include alphas which are very less self correlated or are from different datacategories and data sets.

2) More points to alphas with higher sharpe, low turnover and high sharpe in 2023 period.

3) Will include alphas from all 5 regions to enhance regional diversity.

Suggestions and improvement ideas are highly welcomed.

All the best to everyone for the competition.

### 评论 #12 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 6个月前)

This is a solid and well-structured starting plan, and it already reflects  **portfolio-level thinking** , which is exactly what Osmosis rewards. Your emphasis on low self-correlation and dataset/category diversification is particularly important—many Osmosis underperformances come not from weak individual alphas, but from hidden redundancy across signals that look different on the surface. One refinement I’d suggest is to also monitor  **correlation stability over time** , not just point-in-time correlation, since Osmosis performance can deteriorate quickly when correlations spike during regime shifts.

The second point on prioritizing high Sharpe, low turnover, and strong 2023 performance makes sense, but I’d be cautious about overweighting recent performance alone. It can be useful to check whether those alphas performed well in  *different*  market environments (e.g., volatility expansion vs contraction). Finally, including all five regions is a strong diversification move; just make sure the regional alphas are driven by genuinely local effects rather than globally correlated risk proxies. Overall, this is a disciplined framework—fine-tuning it around regime sensitivity and correlation dynamics could further strengthen it.
