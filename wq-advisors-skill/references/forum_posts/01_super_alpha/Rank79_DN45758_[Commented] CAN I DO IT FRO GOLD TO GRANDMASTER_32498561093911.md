Yes,  **it is possible**  to go  **directly from Gold to GrandMaster**  in the Genius program  **if you meet the necessary criteria** , including:

### 🏆  **1. Pyramids**

  ✅ You're already at  **46**  —  **almost there!**

### 📊  **2. Combined Alpha Performance (CAP)**

- Your  **Combined Alpha Performance (CAP)**  is  **key**  to promotion beyond Gold.
- CAP is a  **black-box metric**  — it reflects how much your alphas contribute positively to the overall Brain pool (OS performance, stability, diversity, risk-adjusted return, etc.).
- Current CAP =  **1.04**  is  **borderline** , and likely  **not enough for GrandMaster**  yet. Most GM consultants report needing  **~1.20+**  consistently.

### ✅  **How to Improve Combined Alpha Performance**

#### **🔹 a. Focus on Quality over Quantity**

- Submitting  **hundreds of alphas**  helps in quantity-based metrics, but for CAP:
  - Each alpha should add  **unique value**  to the pool.
  - Overlapping alphas or minor tweaks often hurt CAP due to redundancy.

#### **🔹 b. Diversify Your Alphas**

- Avoid similar expressions. Explore across themes:
  - **Momentum** : zscores, ranks, ts_delta
  - **Mean Reversion** : neg correlations, ts_min/max
  - **Fundamentals** : e.g., using  `fnd6_*`  cleanly
- **Single-dataset focus**  improves Genius metrics  **and**  stability

#### **🔹 c. Improve Fitness AND Reduce Turnover**

- High fitness matters, but  **CAP favors stable, low-turnover alphas**
- Filter your alpha pool to select:
  - Sharpe > 1.2
  - Turnover < 0.15
  - Fitness > 2.5
- Use tools like  **expression testing**  and  **correlation matrix**

#### **🔹 d. Use Strong Operators Intelligently**

- Underused operators can unlock better alpha structure:
  - Try  `combo_a` ,  `ts_decay` ,  `ts_scale` ,  `delay` ,  `rank` ,  `zscore`
  - Layer them smartly, not just for depth but for  **signal quality**

#### **🔹 e. Weekly Submission Discipline**

- Submit  **5-10 quality alphas weekly**  to benefit from CAP recalculations
- Prune bad ones regularly if they drag CAP down

### ⚠️ Common Pitfalls That Hurt CAP

- Submitting  **similar alphas repeatedly**
- Using  **too many categories or datasets**
- **High-turnover expressions**
- **Excessive tiebreak optimization** —hurts generalizability

### 🛤️ Final Tip: Strategic Patience

- **CAP updates mid and end of each quarter**  — be ready for spikes if you're doing the right things.
- Keep iterating, learning from best submissions, and aim to improve the  **top 20% of your alpha pool** .
