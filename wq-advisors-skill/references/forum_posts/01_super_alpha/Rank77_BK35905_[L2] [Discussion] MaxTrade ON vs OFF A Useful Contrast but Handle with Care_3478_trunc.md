## 帖子正文

Guys, it’s not fair to say MaxTrade OFF is simply “bad.” Switching between ON/OFF can actually help diversify signal discovery, just like toggling universes (TOP3000 vs. TOP1000, MINVOL1M vs. TOPDIV3000).

In practice, though, when many MaxTrade OFF alphas are combined, they often remain concentrated in illiquid names or create abnormal over-trading patterns. A straightforward test is to  **repackage OFF alphas into a MaxTrade ON SuperAlpha and observe**  the residual performance.

Different strategies behave differently. Some actually perform better ON, others better OFF. Sometimes turning ON pushes the alpha toward larger-cap names, since the trading limits are derived from each stock’s historical liquidity. This differentiation is obviously useful — not only for idea generation, but also for boosting your Base Pay (since it depends on the alphas you submit each day).

For example, you might see Sharpe ratios like 2.5/4, 6/9, or 10/12 (in SAs) when comparing ON/OFF. If the gap is too large, that’s a red flag. The real value of OFF is in exploration, while ON tells you if the edge is truly investable. Personally, I like to rotate between both rather than favor one side.

That said, if you prefer a lazy method, you can simply leave ON permanently. This won’t hurt your Combined Performance, and everyone can decide for themselves how much they want to toggle. (jzc
