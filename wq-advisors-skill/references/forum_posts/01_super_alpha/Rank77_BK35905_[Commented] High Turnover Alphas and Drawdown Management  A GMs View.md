## 帖子正文

One often overlooked benefit of high turnover alphas is their ability to smooth drawdowns in a portfolio. Because these signals recycle capital more quickly, they provide  **faster adaptation to changing regimes** , which helps reduce prolonged equity dips.

In my own experience, when I kept submitting mostly mid- to low-turnover alphas and neglected quality control for a month, my Combined PPA OS performance slid from  **2.2 down to 1.9** .

After deliberately adding a set of carefully chosen, unique (low corr) high-turnover alphas, the picture changed: my Combined PPA OS climbed  **back up to**   **2.2** . These alphas didn’t just add raw return; they  **balanced the portfolio dynamics** , offsetting periods when slower alphas struggled. With that said, I’d love to hear your MIT-level professional insights!  (JZC)

**Note:**  Low-turnover Alphas are still better at reducing risk, but high-turnover Alphas can take your solid portfolio to the next level — said by a GM who once hit CAP 3.56 and CSAP 4.6.
