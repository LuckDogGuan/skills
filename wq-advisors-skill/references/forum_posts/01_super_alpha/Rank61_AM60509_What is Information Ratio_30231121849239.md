**Information Coefficient (IC):**  Measures the skill of the manager in predicting returns. It ranges from -1 to 1, with higher values indicating better predictive ability.

**Transfer Coefficient (TC):**  Represents how efficiently the manager translates insights into the portfolio. A TC of 1 means full efficiency, while constraints like transaction costs or risk limits can lower it.

**Breadth (BR):**  Represents the number of independent bets (trades or forecasts) made in a year. More bets improve the chances of overall alpha generation.

**IR(Information ratio)** =IC×TC×sqrt(BR​)

### 评论 #3 (作者: GN51437, 时间: 1年前)

The Information Ratio (IR) measures a manager's ability to generate risk-adjusted excess returns. It is influenced by the Information Coefficient (IC), which reflects the accuracy of return predictions, and Breadth (BR), representing the number of independent investment opportunities. The formula is:

**IR = IC × TC × √BR**

Where:

- **IC (Information Coefficient)**  gauges the predictive skill of the manager.
- **TC (Transfer Coefficient)**  measures how effectively forecasts translate into portfolio positions.
- **BR (Breadth)**  accounts for the number of independent trades made.

A higher IR indicates stronger performance, driven by superior forecasting ability, efficient execution, and a diversified set of investment opportunities.
