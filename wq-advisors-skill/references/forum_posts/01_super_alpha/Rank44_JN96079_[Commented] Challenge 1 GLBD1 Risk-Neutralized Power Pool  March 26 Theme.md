**WorldQuant has announced Challenge 1**  under the  **GLB/D1 Risk-Neutralized Power Pool (PP) Theme** .

🗓  **Competition Timeline:** 
 **March 2, 2026 (12:01 AM EST) – March 15, 2026 (11:59 PM EST)**

### 🌍 Competition Eligibility (Global Leaderboard)

To qualify:

- Create alphas that meet the  **GLB PP Theme**  criteria
- Submit and tag  **at least 5 Pure PP alphas**
- Rankings will be based on a  **common global leaderboard**

### 🌎 Challenge Eligibility (IN / KE / NG / ID Leaderboard)

Participants must:

- Satisfy the Thematic PP Competition Eligibility Criteria
- Maintain  **Alpha Margin > 5 bps**
- Ensure  **Alpha Production Correlation < 0.7**
- Submit alphas across  **at least 5 unique datasets**

### 🏆 Ranking & Tie-Breaker Criteria

- **Primary Ranking:**  Based on  **Maximum Production Correlation**  (Lower is better, must be < 0.7)
- **Tie-Breaker:**  Based on  **Average Production Correlation**  (Lower is better)

This challenge is a great opportunity to focus on  **low-correlation, diversified, production-ready alphas**  within the Risk-Neutralized Power Pool framework.

### 评论 #34 (作者: KP26017, 时间: 3个月前)

**On Risk-Neutralized interpretation in the PP framework:**

Risk neutralization in this context typically means your alpha's returns should not be explainable by standard risk factors — market beta, sector exposure, industry tilts, or market cap bias. The platform neutralizes these systematic exposures so what remains is genuinely idiosyncratic return. This directly influences feature engineering because features that are essentially proxies for sector momentum or market beta will get neutralized away, leaving you with weak residual signal regardless of how strong the raw backtest looked.

Practically this means during feature selection you should pre-neutralize your candidate features yourself before evaluating their predictive power. If a feature loses most of its IC after removing sector and market cap effects, it's not going to survive risk neutralization in production. Test your features in neutralized form from the start rather than discovering the problem after submission.
