The  **Value Factor**  is typically computed over a  **three-month evaluation window** .
For example, the update released on  **25th October 2025**  covers alphas and super alphas submitted during  **June, July, and August** .

This means the most recent Value Factor does  **not**  reflect the latest submissions from the past few weeks but rather those from about  **two to three months earlier** .

The Value Factor is refreshed  **monthly** , and it provides insight into the relative strength and contribution of your alphas over the evaluated period.

### 评论 #10 (作者: SP39437, 时间: 6个月前)

The Value Factor is designed to reflect the medium-term contribution and strength of your submitted Alphas and SuperAlphas rather than very recent activity. It is typically evaluated over a rolling multi-month window, so an update released in a given month often summarizes performance from submissions made several weeks earlier. This lag helps smooth short-term noise and provides a more stable view of which signals consistently add value. The factor is refreshed on a regular monthly schedule and is intended to capture relative quality, persistence, and contribution across that evaluation period, rather than reacting immediately to the latest submissions. As a result, researchers should not expect instant feedback from new ideas, but instead focus on building robust alphas that perform steadily over time. Understanding this timing helps set realistic expectations and encourages long-term thinking instead of short-term optimization. How do you adjust your research workflow knowing the Value Factor reflects delayed, aggregated performance rather than recent submissions?
