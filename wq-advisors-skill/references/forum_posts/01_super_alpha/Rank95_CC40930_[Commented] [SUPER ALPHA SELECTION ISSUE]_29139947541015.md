## 帖子正文

I am writing this post to express my concerns regarding recent changes to data accessibility and the impact they have had on the super alpha simulation process. Specifically, the downgrading of genius level and the removal of others has created significant challenges for those of us relying on these resources for making super alphas.

We could select and run super alphas that contain dataset that is no longer accessible. But after long simulation period, we could not submit those alphas. It’s a huge waste of time.

I would like to suggest that WorldQuant re-evaluate the criteria used to select data for the super alpha. It could save time of consultants and hence improve the efficiency. I propose that the following factors be considered:

- Make sure that all selected data is readily accessible to participants. If some data is not accessible, please raise error from the very first step.
- If you could do it better, please let us to select only alphas that contain accessible datasets (that means alphas that contain unaccessible datasets should be eliminated automatically from the start), without any error.

By making these adjustments, it can help to ensure that consultants could research super alphas more effectively and fosters innovation within the quantitative finance community.

Thank you for your attention to this matter. I look forward to your response.
