### 评论 #8 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 10个月前)

This is a very relevant question because the distinction between PowerPool and regular alphas directly affects GAC strategy. As others mentioned, PowerPool alphas are not officially treated as regular alphas for GAC eligibility, since GAC is meant to reflect standalone alpha quality rather than pooled-signal contributions. However, there are edge cases where PowerPool alphas may appear in GAC scoring if they pass additional regional robustness checks (for example, AMER/EMEA/APAC Sharpe filters). This can make it seem like they qualify, but it’s more an exception than a general rule. The reason is structural: PowerPool signals are designed to diversify pools and improve combo robustness, not necessarily to represent individually tradable strategies. From a best-practice standpoint, if your goal is consistent GAC scoring, you should emphasize regular alphas with broad coverage, balanced turnover, and OS stability. PowerPool submissions can still be valuable for portfolio diversification, but they should be seen more as a complementary stream rather than a reliable path for GAC tiering.
