### 评论 #10 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 5个月前)

For me, a truly robust alpha is defined by a combination of structural consistency, economic intuition, and practical survivability rather than any single metric. Consistent out-of-sample Sharpe is necessary, but not sufficient on its own. I pay close attention to how the alpha behaves across different universes, delays, and market regimes, because signals that only work in one narrow setting are often overfit to specific microstructures. Robustness also shows up as low sensitivity to small parameter changes—if a slight tweak in decay, lookback, or universe causes the signal to collapse, that’s a red flag. Investability matters as well: an alpha that looks great before maxtrade, turnover, or transaction constraints but degrades sharply afterward is not truly robust. Finally, I value regime resilience—signals that may weaken but do not invert during volatility spikes, liquidity stress, or trend reversals tend to be the ones that survive long-term competition and crowding pressure.
