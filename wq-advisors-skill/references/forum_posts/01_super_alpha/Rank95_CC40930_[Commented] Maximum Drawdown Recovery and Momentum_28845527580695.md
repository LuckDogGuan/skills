### Abstract:

This study empirically tests asset price predictability using stock selection rules based on maximum drawdown and its consecutive recovery. In various equity markets, portfolios constructed from these criteria exhibit superior performance in forecasting asset price directions and capturing cross-sectional return differentials. The findings suggest that incorporating drawdown and recovery metrics can enhance momentum and contrarian strategies, leading to improved risk-adjusted returns.

**Author** : Jaehyung Choi
 **Published** : March 31, 2014
 **Link** :  [arXiv:1403.8125](https://arxiv.org/abs/1403.8125)

I'm excited to share a compelling research paper that examines how drawdown and recovery metrics can be leveraged for alpha generation:

**"Maximum Drawdown, Recovery, and Momentum"**  by Jaehyung Choi

This study investigates stock selection strategies based on maximum drawdown and subsequent recovery, revealing that such criteria can enhance momentum and contrarian approaches across various equity markets. The insights provided could be invaluable for developing robust alpha-generating strategies.

I found the integration of drawdown metrics into alpha creation particularly insightful. Looking forward to discussing its implications and hearing your thoughts!
