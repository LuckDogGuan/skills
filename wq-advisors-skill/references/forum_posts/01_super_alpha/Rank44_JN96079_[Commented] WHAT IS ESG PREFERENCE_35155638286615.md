## 帖子正文

**ESG Preference**  in WorldQuant refers to a quantitative factor or alpha signal that captures investor sentiment or market behavior related to Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) considerations. As ESG factors increasingly influence investment decisions, WorldQuant integrates ESG Preference to identify how markets react to companies with varying ESG ratings or trends.

This preference can be derived from alternative data, such as ESG rating changes, controversies, carbon disclosures, or social impact news. By analyzing these data points, WorldQuant aims to quantify how ESG-related developments affect stock prices or market dynamics.

For example, a positive change in ESG score may predict future outperformance if markets reward responsible practices. Conversely, ESG controversies may lead to underperformance. ESG Preference is used alongside other alphas in WorldQuant’s systematic investment models to enhance risk-adjusted returns while capturing the growing impact of ESG factors on global financial markets.

### 评论 #5 (作者: TP85668, 时间: 9个月前)

This is a really important question because turnover directly affects both transaction costs and the stability of alpha performance. In practice, I start by evaluating the overall market regime: during high-volatility phases, allowing slightly higher turnover can help capture short-lived signals, whereas in calmer or illiquid markets, keeping turnover lower avoids unnecessary costs and slippage. Another useful step is to test across different universes and neutralization settings—sometimes an alpha looks fine in one setup but turnover explodes in another. I also like to benchmark turnover against liquidity measures (like ADV or sector averages) to ensure the trades are realistic. Ultimately, there’s no universal “ideal” rate, but combining market context, backtesting, and robustness checks usually helps identify a sustainable balance.
