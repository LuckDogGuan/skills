### 评论 #3 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 10个月前)

The Global Alpha Competition (GAC) typically evaluates alphas on multiple dimensions, with the primary criteria being predictive power, robustness, and implementability. An eligible alpha must meet Genius submission requirements, including passing syntactic validation, producing finite outputs, and adhering to constraints on runtime, data fields, and operators. Performance is usually assessed using a combination of in-sample and out-of-sample tests, with metrics like IR (Information Ratio) and stability over different time horizons. In addition, alphas should avoid excessive turnover to ensure they are realistic under transaction cost assumptions. Correlation with existing high-performing signals is also monitored—low correlation to the existing pool increases the alpha’s value. Finally, to remain eligible, an alpha must be original (not plagiarized), comply with the platform’s terms, and be submitted through approved methods within the competition period.

### 评论 #6 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 10个月前)

In the Global Alpha Competition (GAC), the eligibility of an alpha is based on both submission rules and performance criteria. Any alpha you submit during the competition period is considered for scoring as long as it runs successfully on the platform, passes basic validation, and meets the competition’s data and universe requirements. There is no fixed limit to the number of alphas you can submit, but the scoring focuses on both the quality and quantity of your eligible alphas. High-ranking alphas typically show strong out-of-sample performance, robustness across multiple universes, and low correlation to existing production signals. While high raw performance helps, uniqueness and incremental value to the production set are equally important. In the later stages, top participants are also evaluated on alpha generation skill, model diversity, and originality. Therefore, the best approach is to design robust, low-correlation alphas that complement the current production portfolio rather than duplicate common patterns.

### 评论 #9 (作者: AG14039, 时间: 9个月前)

The Global Alpha Competition (GAC) evaluates alphas across several key dimensions, primarily focusing on predictive power, robustness, and implementability. To be eligible, an alpha must meet Genius submission requirements, including passing syntactic checks, generating finite outputs, and complying with limits on runtime, data fields, and operators. Performance is assessed through both in-sample and out-of-sample tests, using metrics like Information Ratio and stability across different timeframes. Alphas should also maintain reasonable turnover to remain realistic under transaction cost assumptions, and lower correlation with existing high-performing signals increases their value. Finally, all submissions must be original, comply with platform rules, and be submitted via approved methods within the competition period.
