## 帖子正文

Hi everyone,

I'm curious if having a high Daily Osmosis Rank usually translates to a high Combined Osmosis. Based on my observations over the past few months, they actually seem to be inversely related.

Specifically:

- Consultants who consistently maintain a Combined Osmosis > 2 across months usually hover around a much lower Daily rank (about 0.5).
- Conversely, those with exceptionally high Daily ranks rarely achieved a high Combined score.

Has anyone else noticed this trend or can explain the mechanics behind it? Would love to hear your thoughts!

### 评论 #1 (作者: JO96892, 时间: 1个月前)

No, they are different by design. A  **high Daily Rank**  is a snapshot of one day’s performance, while Combined Osmosis measures consistent, long-term stability over a full quarter.

Why They Differ

**Daily Osmosis Rank:** This metric is a relative ranking of one day's PnL from two years ago against other consultants.

**Combined Osmosis:** This measures your long-term  **Sharpe ratio** , which is a score of how steady your  **returns**  are. It heavily penalizes big  **drawdowns** .

The inverse relationship you see is likely because consultants with high Combined scores prioritize  **robustness**  and  **diversification** across dataset, category, operator and neutralization to minimize risk. They use a diversified approach to keep returns steady. In contrast, those chasing a high Daily Rank often have concentrated combos that create big swings therefore resulting volatility usually crushes their long-term score.

### 评论 #8 (作者: KP26017, 时间: 1个月前)

Daily Osmosis Rank reflects your relative position among all active consultants on a specific day — it's a competitive ranking that depends heavily on who else is active and submitting on that particular day. A high daily rank means you outperformed other consultants on that specific day's evaluation, which can be driven by short-term factors — a single alpha performing well, submitting on a low-competition day, or catching a favorable regime for your specific signal types.
