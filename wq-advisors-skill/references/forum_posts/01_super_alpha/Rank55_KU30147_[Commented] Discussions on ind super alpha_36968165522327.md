### 评论 #9 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 6个月前)

High prod correlation in IND super alphas is a very common structural issue on WorldQuant Brain, and your diagnosis is largely correct. The IND universe is relatively small, liquidity is uneven, and the neutralization space is limited, so many independently “reasonable” alphas end up expressing the same dominant effects. When you build a super alpha on top of your own regular alphas—especially if they share horizons, operator families, or risk controls—the aggregation naturally amplifies overlap rather than diversifying it, which explains why prod correlation quickly exceeds 0.7.

When the pool of regular alphas is small, the most effective lever is behavioral differentiation, not more combinations. Instead of mixing similar signals, try constructing base alphas that deliberately differ along one axis: time horizon (fast vs slow), transformation type (ts-based vs cross-sectional), or intent (directional vs conditioning). For combo expressions, I’ve seen better results using  *orthogonal logic*  such as interaction-style combinations (e.g., one alpha gating or modulating another) rather than linear blends. In IND, fewer but more structurally distinct components usually reduce prod correlation more reliably than expanding the alpha count mechanically.
