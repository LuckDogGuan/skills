### 评论 #10 (作者: HY45205, 时间: 1年前)

Hi SV78590,

Thank you for opening up this discussion on improving the value factor—an important yet often challenging aspect to optimize. Here are some suggestions that might help:

### 1.  **Focus on Metrics Beyond the Basics**

While traditional value metrics like P/E or P/B are effective, consider incorporating newer or less commonly used indicators, such as:

- **Earnings Yield** : Calculated as earnings divided by enterprise value, which adjusts for debt.
- **Free Cash Flow Yield** : Free cash flow divided by enterprise value, providing insights into a company's operational efficiency.
- **Economic Value Added (EVA)** : Captures the company's ability to generate returns above its cost of capital.

### 2.  **Region-Specific Customization**

Different markets react to value metrics differently. For instance, emerging markets may show stronger responses to high dividend yields, whereas developed markets might favor lower P/B ratios. Try tailoring your metrics to the regions you target.

Hi ,

Super Alpha primarily affects the weight factor, as it determines how weights are distributed across your portfolio. This impact can be positive if the Super Alpha enhances diversification and reduces concentration. Here's how the factors are influenced:

**Value Factor** :

- Ensure the Super Alpha is well-diversified. Check that self-correlation and product correlation are below the pool average and continue to decrease over time.
- Maintain strong out-of-sample (OS) performance to validate the alpha's quality.

**Weight Factor** :

- After approximately six months, profitable alphas are assigned higher weights.
- The scaling process is gradual (e.g., starting from 0 → 0.1 → 1 → 10).
- Exercise patience as the alpha's weight increases over time.
