**Tips for getting started with  [Macro](https://platform.worldquantbrain.com/data/data-sets?category=macro)  Datasets:**

- The macro data category encompasses diverse datasets covering macroeconomic research, job market analytics, technical stock ratings, and more. It enables the identification of economic trends, shifts in market sentiment, and assessments of companies' financial stability.
- Macro data on US economic indicators or interest rates can be an excellent indicator to detect market regimes, helping to determine whether the economy is in a phase of expansion or contraction.

**Example Alpha Ideas:**

1. During economic expansion, going long on cyclical stocks identified by high beta or high earnings volatility can be advantageous, while defensive stocks may be more favorable during economic contraction.
2. Company expansion can be a positive indicator of financial health. Consider going long on stocks of companies that have recently increased their job postings compared to their peers in the same industry.

**Recommended Datasets:**

- [Macroeconomic Indicators](https://platform.worldquantbrain.com/data/data-sets/macro4)
- [Job records from job posting](https://platform.worldquantbrain.com/data/data-sets/macro27)
- [Technical Ratings Model](https://platform.worldquantbrain.com/data/data-sets/macro38)

Hi,

Macro datasets provide insights into broader economic trends and market conditions. They include information on economic indicators like GDP, interest rates, job market data, and company performance metrics, helping to understand the overall economic environment.

For example, macroeconomic data such as US economic indicators or interest rates can reveal whether the economy is in an expansion phase (growth) or contraction (slowdown). Recognizing these shifts helps identify the best investment strategies.

**Alpha Ideas** :

- **Economic Expansion** : When the economy is growing, it’s often a good time to invest in  **cyclical stocks** , which are more sensitive to economic changes, like those with high beta or earnings volatility.
- **Economic Contraction** : During slowdowns,  **defensive stocks**  (e.g., utilities or healthcare) tend to perform better because they’re less affected by economic changes.
- **Company Expansion** : Look for companies increasing job postings, which can signal growth and financial stability.

**Recommended Datasets** :

- Macroeconomic Indicators
- Job Records from Job Postings
- Technical Ratings Models
