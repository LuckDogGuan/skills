If your  **CAP and CSAP are dropping** , it’s often due to alpha redundancy, low-quality signals, or overused logic. Try these quick fixes:

1. **Use different logic types**  (e.g., volatility, fragility, regime-based) to reduce correlation.

3. **Diversify datasets and regions**  to improve contribution.
4. **Keep alphas simple and stable**  — low operator count, avoid overfitting.
5. **Regularly retire stale or similar alphas**  from your portfolio.

A few strong, unique signals go further than many similar ones. #CAP #CSAP #AlphaQuality

### 评论 #14 (作者: ST50816, 时间: 1年前)

### 1.  **Improve Individual Alpha Quality**

- **Sharpen your IC (Information Coefficient):**  Aim for higher correlation between your signal and future returns.
- **Reduce noise:**  Use smoothing techniques like EWMA, or normalize inputs to reduce outliers.
- **Backtest robustness:**  Avoid overfitting. Use walk-forward analysis, multiple regimes, and cross-validation.
- **Use strong predictive features:**  Go beyond basic price-volume data—incorporate alternative data if allowed (sentiment, fundamentals, options data).
- ### 2.  **Focus on Orthogonality**
  - **Low correlation is key:**  Combine alphas that don’t move together. A bunch of similar signals won’t add value.
  - Use  **PCA (Principal Component Analysis)**  or  **clustering**  to measure signal overlap.
  - Submit signals from  **diverse domains** : some price-based, others fundamental, machine-learned, seasonal, etc.
  - ### 3.  **Weight Alphas Intelligently**
  - **Optimize combination weights** : Equal weight rarely works best. Use:
  - **Mean-variance optimization**
  - **IC-weighting**
  - **Hierarchical Risk Parity (HRP)**  for stability
  - **Dynamically update weights**  over time based on signal decay, regime shifts, or volatility.
