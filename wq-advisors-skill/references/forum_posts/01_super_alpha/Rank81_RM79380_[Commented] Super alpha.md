### 评论 #2 (作者: TK60163, 时间: 2个月前)

[SS35873](/hc/en-us/profiles/30754829898903-SS35873)  How so? Why would weight factor and value factor depend on alpha submitted? As someone who doesn't have any weight factor, I can't give my opinion on this matter but for value factor, I don't think submitting many alphas will help and here's why:

The Value Factor can be summed up by these factors:
1. Individual alpha performance;
2. Diversity of recent alpha submissions;
3. Uniqueness of submissions as compared to your past submissions and
those submissions of other part-time consultants

When submitting many alphas, sometimes we are forced to submit alphas that have high production correlation just so that we can meet the 4 alphas criteria, this causes the downfall of diversity. Next, uniqueness of submissions as compared to past submissions will get better if lesser alphas are submitted. Imagine you only submitted 5 alphas with (<0.2 prod corr and self corr) for 2 quarters. The uniqueness is going to be really high. However, if you submitted 200 alphas each in 2 quarters that ranges from (0.1<x<0.5 prod corr and self corr) the uniqueness is lower than the 5 alphas in 2 quarters as the average correlation increases.

I would like your thoughts on this since this is from my own understanding of how Value Factor is computed, thank you.
