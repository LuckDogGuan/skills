### 评论 #4 (作者: 顾问 JN96079 (Rank 44), 时间: 5个月前)

That’s an interesting insight, and I’ve come to a similar conclusion over time. Turnover by itself doesn’t really  *create*  returns—it mainly affects costs—while volatility is much closer to the engine of return potential, especially in pooled or scaled portfolios like PowerPool. Shifting focus toward higher-volatility alphas can make sense as long as they’re well-behaved: stable Sharpe, controlled tails, and predictable turnover so costs don’t explode. In practice, I’ve found the sweet spot isn’t “high volatility at any cost,” but alphas where volatility reflects genuine information flow rather than noise. Those tend to scale better in PowerPool than ultra-smooth, low-volatility signals that simply don’t move enough to contribute meaningfully.
