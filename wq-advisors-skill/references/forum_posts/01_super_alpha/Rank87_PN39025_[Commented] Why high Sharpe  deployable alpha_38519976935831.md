## 帖子正文

A high Sharpe ratio in backtests does not automatically mean an alpha is deployable in live markets. Sharpe measures risk-adjusted returns under idealized assumptions, but  **it often ignores real-world frictions like transaction costs, slippage, market impact, and capacity constraints** . Many high-Sharpe signals achieve their performance through high turnover or concentrated exposures, which become expensive or unstable when scaled. Additionally, a strong in-sample Sharpe can reflect overfitting to historical noise rather than true predictive power.  **A deployable alpha must remain robust after costs, maintain performance across sub-universes and time periods, and demonstrate stability under realistic execution assumptions**   - not just look good on a backtest report.
