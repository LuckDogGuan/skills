To improve both combined alpha performance and combined selected alpha performance, we should adopt a structured approach focusing on diversification, robustness, and risk management.

First, producing low-correlation alphas is crucial to reducing redundancy and enhancing overall portfolio stability. Spreading alphas across different regions further minimizes geographic concentration risks and improves resilience to local market shocks.

Second, submitted alphas must have a high Sharpe ratio, significantly exceeding the required thresholds, ensuring strong risk-adjusted returns. Utilizing diverse datasets while avoiding overfitting helps maintain the generalizability and long-term viability of alphas.

Lastly, prioritizing a high return/drawdown ratio enhances capital efficiency and mitigates downside risk. By combining these principles, we can develop a more resilient and effective alpha portfolio.
