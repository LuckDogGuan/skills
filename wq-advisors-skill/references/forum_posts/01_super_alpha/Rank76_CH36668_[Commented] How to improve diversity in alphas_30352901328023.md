### 评论 #5 (作者: KS69567, 时间: 1年前)

Improving alpha diversity enhances robustness and reduces correlation risks. Here are key strategies:

1. **Vary Data Sources**  – Use diverse datasets beyond price and volume, such as fundamentals, sentiment, options flow, or alternative data.
2. **Different Factor Classes**  – Combine momentum, value, growth, volatility, and liquidity-based factors to reduce redundancy.
3. **Cross-Timeframe Signals**  – Mix short-term, medium-term, and long-term signals to capture different market behaviors.
4. **Regional & Market Segmentation**  – Develop alphas across different sectors, and market caps.
5. **Structural Variations**  – Use different mathematical transformations.
6. **Execution & Turnover Adjustments**  – Include both high- and low-frequency alphas to balance risk and return.

By incorporating these approaches, you can build a more resilient and decorrelated alpha portfolio.

Value-related factors, such as the price-to-book and price-to-earnings ratios
Momentum factors, such as price momentum and relative strength
Volatility factors, such as beta and historical volatility
Sentiment factors, such as sentiment on social media and in the news

Grouping Factors: Look at the factors you have been employing in each area. They can then be grouped according to data sources or construction similarities. ### 评论 #23 (作者: SP39437, 时间: 1年前)

To effectively integrate regular datasets with new ones, start by applying  **custom neutralizations**  to account for sector, industry, or market cap biases. This ensures that the alpha signals are not dominated by any specific group and remain broadly applicable. Next,  **design entry and exit conditions**  using  **alternative datasets**  such as sentiment analysis, earnings surprises, or macroeconomic indicators, which can provide additional insights beyond traditional price-based signals.

To extract the most relevant insights from new datasets, use basic operations like  **comparison of estimates vs. actuals** , which can highlight mispricings or overreactions. Furthermore, clustering techniques like  **K-means**  or  **HDBSCAN**  can be applied to group similar alphas, identify redundancies, and promote diversification within your alpha pool. This helps explore less-explored factor spaces and ensures the inclusion of non-correlated signals, improving the overall robustness and performance of your alpha strategy.
