### 评论 #2 (作者: AG14039, 时间: 9个月前)

In the Merged Alpha Performance Competition (MAPC), the IS (In-Sample) and OS (Out-of-Sample) scores evaluate alpha performance across different periods. The IS score measures how well an alpha performs during the in-sample period, using metrics like Sharpe ratio, returns, and turnover-adjusted ratios, reflecting historical effectiveness. The OS score assesses robustness by testing the alpha on out-of-sample data, ensuring it generalizes well and isn’t overfitted to past patterns. Only regular alphas in the GLOBAL universe with Delay 1 and proper risk-neutralization are eligible, while Power Pool and SuperAlphas are excluded. Each alpha’s IS and OS metrics are computed and then aggregated to produce the overall MAPC rankings.

### 评论 #6 (作者: AS13853, 时间: 9个月前)

hello thanks for such information ,In the Merged Alpha Performance Competition (MAPC), alpha scoring is based on both In-Sample (IS) and Out-of-Sample (OS) performance. The total score is calculated using the formula: Total Score = 0.75 × OS + 0.25 × IS. The IS score measures how well an alpha performs during the in-sample period using metrics like Sharpe ratio, returns, and turnover. The OS score tests the alpha’s ability to generalize to unseen data, ensuring robustness and avoiding overfitting. Only regular GLOBAL universe Delay-1 alphas with proper risk-neutralization are eligible, while PowerPool and SuperAlphas are excluded from the competition.
