### 评论 #7 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 1年前)

The Value Factor is based on the principle that  **stocks with lower relative prices compared to their fundamental value tend to outperform over time** . Some common value metrics include:

- **Price-to-Book Ratio (P/B)**  → Lower values indicate potential undervaluation.
- **Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E)**  → A lower P/E suggests the stock is cheaper relative to earnings.
- **Price-to-Sales Ratio (P/S)**  → Helps evaluate companies with lower earnings but stable revenue.
- **Enterprise Value-to-EBITDA (EV/EBITDA)**  → Measures valuation relative to operating earnings.
- **Dividend Yield**  → Higher dividend yields may signal undervaluation.
