## My Alpha Selection Criteria

### 1. Don’t Obsess Over IS Metrics

This is probably my biggest difference from most people.

I  **don’t care too much about IS metrics** . As long as an alpha doesn’t have obvious fatal flaws, I will still consider submitting it.

The  **first and most important check**  is:

- **PnL over the past two years must be upward**
- No large historical crashes or extreme drawdowns

Only after passing this do I consider submission.

### 2. Diversity Matters More Than “Looking Good”

The second key point is  **diversity** .

I don’t only submit alphas that look “good” according to common standards.

I believe that if the alphas submitted are  **too similar in one region** , the resulting combination will inevitably perform poorly.

This idea comes from  **high-school biology** :
👉  **Biodiversity is critical to ecosystem stability** .

I think this type of reasoning applies to  **most systems** , including alpha portfolios.

Therefore, after submitting some relatively similar alphas, I will deliberately select and submit a few  **riskier, more unique alphas** .

### Final Takeaway

- Avoid overfitting > chasing clever expressions
- Simple, first-order alphas can still be diverse
- Recent PnL trend and portfolio diversity matter more than pretty IS numbers
- Sometimes,  **imperfect but different**  alphas are exactly what your portfolio needs

## 讨论与评论 (1)

### 评论 #1 (作者: CM46415, 时间: 2个月前)

Strong perspective—especially the emphasis on  **diversification over “pretty” IS metrics** .

In practice, Value Factor improves most when you:

- Avoid overfitting to IS Sharpe and focus on  **stable PnL behavior**
- Ensure each alpha adds  **new information (low correlation)**
- Prioritize  **portfolio-level diversity**  instead of individual “perfect” signals
- Accept that slightly weaker but unique alphas can improve overall VF more than similar high-performing ones

This aligns well with real portfolio construction thinking—robustness and diversity usually matter more than isolated performance spikes.
