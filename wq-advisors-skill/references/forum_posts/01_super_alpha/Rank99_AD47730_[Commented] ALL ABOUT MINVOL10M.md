## 帖子正文

**MINVOL10M**  refers to a liquidity filter that selects stocks with a minimum average daily trading volume of about 10 million units (usually dollars traded or shares traded, depending on the dataset definition). It is commonly used when creating universes for alpha development and backtesting. The purpose of MINVOL10M is to ensure that selected stocks are sufficiently liquid, meaning they can be traded efficiently with lower transaction costs and reduced market impact.

Using MINVOL10M helps improve strategy robustness because illiquid stocks often produce noisy or unrealistic alpha signals. Highly liquid stocks also provide more reliable pricing data and smoother execution in simulations. In practice, this filter narrows the universe to actively traded securities, often including large-cap and mid-cap companies. Quant researchers prefer such universes because results are more stable, scalable, and closer to real-world implementation. It also reduces survivorship bias and minimizes distortions caused by extreme price movements in thinly traded stocks.
