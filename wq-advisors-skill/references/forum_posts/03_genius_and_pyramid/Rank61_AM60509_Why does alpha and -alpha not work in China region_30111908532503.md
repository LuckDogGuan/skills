### 评论 #4 (作者: MA97359, 时间: 1年前)

In the China region,  **alpha and -alpha**  may not work as expected due to several structural and market-specific factors:

1. **Market Asymmetry & Regulatory Influence**  – The Chinese market is heavily influenced by government policies, state-owned enterprises, and interventions, leading to structural imbalances where long and short signals behave differently.
2. **Short-Selling Restrictions**  – Unlike developed markets, China has strict short-selling regulations, limited borrow availability, and higher costs, making it difficult to execute  **-alpha**  strategies effectively.
3. **Retail-Dominated Market**  – A large portion of trading volume comes from retail investors, often driven by sentiment and speculation, making price movements less symmetric and weakening the assumption that  **alpha and -alpha should behave oppositely** .
4. **Liquidity & Execution Differences**  – Certain stocks, especially in A-shares, have liquidity constraints, with  **upward and downward price moves behaving differently** , impacting the efficiency of long vs. short signals.
5. **Factor Asymmetry**  – Some signals (e.g., momentum, mean reversion) work well on the long side but fail on the short side due to unique market microstructure, capital controls, and periodic policy shifts.

In short,  **alpha ≠ -alpha in China**  due to asymmetric risks, regulatory constraints, and market inefficiencies that affect long and short positions differently.
