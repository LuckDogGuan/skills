#### **What Are Financial Ratios, and Why are they important?**

#### Financial ratios are tools that help investors evaluate a company’s financial performance. They simplify complex data into actionable insights by highlighting key aspects like profitability, valuation, and stability.

***Below are some examples of financial ratios to get started on:***

- **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**

***What It Measures:***  A company's valuation relative to its earnings.

Low P/E ratios may indicate undervalued stocks. A strategy could involve identifying stocks with below-average P/E ratios in an industry and holding them until their valuation aligns with the market.

- **Return on Equity (ROE)**

***What It Measures*** : A company’s profitability relative to shareholders’ equity.
High ROE often signals efficient management. An alpha strategy could focus on selecting companies with consistently high ROE over a specific period.

- **Debt-to-Equity (D/E) Ratio**

***What It Measures:*** The proportion of debt financing relative to equity.
Companies with low D/E ratios may be financially stable. Your alpha could involve shortlisting such companies during economic downturns when stability is rewarded.

- **Dividend Yield**

***What It Measures:*** A company’s annual dividend payments as a percentage of its stock price.
 Focus on high-dividend-yield stocks during low-interest-rate environments to benefit from income-focused investors’ demand.
