You can try these:

1. Quantitative Equity Portfolio Management: Modern Techniques and Applications. Edward E. Qian

## 讨论与评论 (7)

### 评论 #1 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 9个月前)

Great recommendations! Each of these books provides a different layer of understanding that is directly useful for alpha research. Qian’s  *Quantitative Equity Portfolio Management*  is excellent for bridging theoretical models with practical portfolio construction—it helps explain why diversification and risk control matter just as much as raw signal generation. Tan’s  *Asian Financial Statement Analysis*  is a fantastic resource for anyone exploring fundamentals in regions like CHN or ASI, where local accounting practices and irregularities can create unique alpha opportunities. And Hull’s  *Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives*  remains a cornerstone for understanding derivatives pricing and risk management, even if it’s dense. I’d also suggest complementing these with  *Active Portfolio Management*  by Grinold & Kahn for a more performance attribution–focused view, and  *Advances in Financial Machine Learning*  by Marcos López de Prado for modern data-driven techniques. Together, these create a comprehensive toolkit that can directly inform both theoretical insights and practical alpha construction.
