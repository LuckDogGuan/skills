### 评论 #2 (作者: DT49505, 时间: 1年前)

Hi 顾问 MS18311 (Rank 70), Combined Alpha Performance (CAP) and Combined Selected Alpha Performance (CSAP) are two of the most critical metrics in WorldQuant’s Genius framework. CAP typically reflects the cumulative performance of all your alphas when combined, while CSAP refers to the subset of your alphas that are selected for live combination, often those with the strongest contribution metrics. The calculation of CAP factors in returns, turnover, and correlation, among other metrics. To improve CAP, focus on building diversified alphas that are individually performant but not highly correlated to one another. Lowering average pairwise correlation across your alphas increases the likelihood that their combination will yield a stronger, more stable portfolio. Additionally, consider increasing the number of unique pyramids and tuning parameters such as operator use and field diversity to reduce overfitting. Studying top performers with CSAP above 3, as 顾问 PN39025 (Rank 87) suggested, can give you practical insights into what types of logic structures and data sources are most effective.

### 评论 #6 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 1年前)

Great question. To improve  **Combined Alpha Performance (CAP)**  and  **Combined Selected Alpha Performance (CSAP)** , the key lies in building  **diverse and orthogonal alphas** . While CAP aggregates all your alphas, CSAP focuses on those selected for live use—often your top performers. Both metrics consider  **Sharpe ratio** ,  **turnover** ,  **correlation** , and  **stability** . To boost these, aim for alphas with Sharpe > 1.5, low correlation (especially average pairwise < 0.05), and different logic trees. You can also enhance diversity using varied universes, operators, and features, and applying  `group_neutralize`  or  `rescale` . Regularly review your alpha set to eliminate highly correlated or underperforming signals. The more orthogonal and consistent your alphas, the stronger your CAP and CSAP will be.
