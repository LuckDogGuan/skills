## 📊 Step 1: Fetch Genius Board Data

**Objective:**  Gather detailed data from the Genius leaderboard to gain an accurate, comprehensive overview of the current status.

**Detailed Concept:**

- **Data Source:**  Retrieve information from the official Genius board, including user profiles, the number of alphas submitted, submission times, and other relevant indicators at the end-of-quarter phase.
- **Benefits:**  A complete database allows for the analysis of trends and objective assessment of each user's performance.
- **Challenges:**  Ensure continuous, accurate data updates to avoid errors or missing information.

## 🔎 Step 2: Filter Users by Submitted Alpha Count

**Objective:**  Focus on users who have submitted around 20 alphas—the minimum required to meet the quarterly submission window of 20 days.

**Detailed Concept:**

- **Filtering Criteria:**  Select users with a minimum alpha count (e.g., around 20 alphas) to ensure they are actively engaged and eligible for the rerank pool.
- **Analysis:**  This filtering helps eliminate cases with insufficient or subpar activity, ensuring more reliable data for further analysis.

## ✅ Step 3: Filter Users Based on 3 Eligibility Criteria and Slot Allocation for Each Rank

**Objective:**  Classify users who meet the minimum criteria for each rank.

**Detailed Concept:**

- **Slot Allocation for Each Rank:**
  - **Gold:**  70%
  - **Expert:**  20%
  - **Master:**  8%
  - **GM:**  2%
- **Value of This Criterion:**  This step processes the minimum value data for each rank, ensuring that only qualified candidates are considered for each tier.

## ⚖️ Step 4: Calculate Tiebreak Using Normalized Data

**Objective:**  For users with equivalent metrics, use data normalization methods to fairly determine their ranking.

**Detailed Concept:**

- **Process:**
  - **Data Normalization:**  Adjust all indicators to a common scale to eliminate bias from time-dependent or individual-specific circumstances.
  - **Comparison and Ranking:**  Apply a tiebreak model that factors in metrics such as Operators per Alpha and Operators Used to determine the final order.
- **Benefits:**  This ensures a transparent and objective ranking process by reducing the influence of external variables.

## 🎯 Step 5: Calculate the Probability for Consultants to Achieve Their Desired Rank & Provide Improvement Advice

**Objective:**  Develop a predictive model based on the processed data to estimate each consultant’s probability of reaching their target rank.

**Detailed Concept:**

- **Probability Analysis:**
  - **Predictive Modeling:**  Utilize historical data and current parameters (alpha count, pyramid/combine performance, tiebreak criteria) to calculate the likelihood of achieving a specific rank.
  - **Incorporate Tiebreak Factors:**  Adjust tiebreak metrics and improvement trends quarter by quarter to generate a more accurate forecast.
- **Improvement Advice:**
  - If the probability of achieving the desired rank is low, propose strategies to enhance performance—such as increasing alpha count, optimizing submission quality, or learning from successful consultants.
  - Engage in personalized strategy discussions, analyzing strengths and weaknesses, and consider seeking guidance from experienced mentors or the community.
