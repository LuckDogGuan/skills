### 评论 #52 (作者: KP26017, 时间: 3个月前)

Really important conversation to have and the overcrowding problem is more real than most people acknowledge openly.

The clustering around momentum, reversion, and volume anomalies isn't surprising — they're well documented, easier to implement, and show up clearly in backtests. But that's precisely why their edge has compressed and why correlation within the pool is so high. Everyone fishing in the same pond.

**Your three directions are solid choices:**

Reporting cycle data is genuinely underexplored in most community pools. The timing dimension of when information arrives — not just what the information says — carries independent signal. Earnings announcement timing relative to fiscal year end, delay patterns in filing, consistency of reporting cadence — these behavioral patterns around disclosure often predict subsequent returns in ways that the reported numbers themselves don't.

Capital structure changes are excellent precisely because they require more fundamental analysis to interpret correctly. Debt issuance, buyback initiation, dividend initiations and cuts, convertible offerings — each carries distinct signaling content that isn't captured by price or volume at all. The market reaction to these events is also often delayed in smaller names giving you execution runway.
