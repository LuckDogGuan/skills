> How comes that sometimes when I submit one more alpha, the total IS score decreased?

The score may decrease if a new alpha submitted is not improving the merged performance of your alpha pool. For example, if you submit an alpha with a low Sharpe ratio, low return and high turnover, your overall merged performance may decrease.

Please check out this post Understanding  [Modern Portfolio Theory to Enhance MAPC Score]([L2] Understanding Modern Portfolio Theory to Enhance MAPC ScoreResearch_24558442254615.md)  - it is helpful for ATOM too, as the ATOM scoring is based on merged performance of all your alphas submitted on or after October 7, 2024

### 评论 #23 (作者: AG20578, 时间: 1年前)

Hi  [DB89987](/hc/en-us/profiles/1532394604362-DB89987) !

> Why is it so difficult to make GLB alphas? Any suggestions on what could help us create GLB alphas?

I would recommend to use appropriate groupings for GLB region. GLB has different countries inside (you can check them out by using Visualization tool). So try to neutralize your alpha to combination of country/exchange and sector/industry. To combine groups you can use new operator group_cartesian_product(group1, group2). For example group_cartesian_product(country, industry) and use it as group in other group_ operator:

```
group = group_cartesian_product(country, industry); group_zscore(alpha, group)
```

Also try MINVOL1M universe and Risk Neutralizations.

### 评论 #28 (作者: AG20578, 时间: 1年前)

Hi  [顾问 AM60509 (Rank 61)](/hc/en-us/profiles/25127049757975-顾问 AM60509 (Rank 61)) !

> how to create alphas on GLB region using ESG factors?What operators can be used?

Operators depends on the datatset and on the idea behind the alpha. If data is vector type, start with a vec_ operator. Next, you could use factors directly or combine them into a composite factor. Be sure to neutralize your alpha to account for differences across countries or sectors. The exact approach will depend on your hypothesis about ESG factors and stock performance.
