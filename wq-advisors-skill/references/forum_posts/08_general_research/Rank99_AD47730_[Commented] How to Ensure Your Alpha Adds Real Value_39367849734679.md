## 帖子正文

Hello everyone,

While experimenting, I often feel like I’m just tweaking the same idea rather than creating something genuinely new.

1. How do you determine if a newly built alpha is truly distinct from your existing ones?
2. Do you rely mainly on correlation metrics, or are there other checks you use?
3. Are there any practical methods to identify whether an idea adds real incremental value?
4. How do you avoid falling into the trap of generating repetitive variations?

Appreciate your insights. Thank you!

## 讨论与评论 (1)

### 评论 #1 (作者: 顾问 AD47730 (Rank 99), 时间: 1个月前)

Check correlation, but also compare logic, data fields, holding period, turnover, decay, and behavior across regimes. A distinct alpha should improve portfolio fitness, reduce drawdown, or add stability—not just look different syntactically.
