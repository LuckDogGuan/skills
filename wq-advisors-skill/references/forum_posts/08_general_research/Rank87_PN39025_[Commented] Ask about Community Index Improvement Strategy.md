### 评论 #5 (作者: NT84064, 时间: 10个月前)

The Community Index (or community score) isn’t just a raw count of your posts or comments — it’s influenced by the quality, relevance, and engagement your contributions generate over time. The algorithm may weigh factors such as how many people interact with your comments (likes, follows, replies), whether your posts attract sustained engagement, and whether your activity is consistent across different content types (comments, original posts, answering questions). It’s also possible that scores decay naturally over time to encourage ongoing contribution, meaning you need to offset this decay with fresh, high-quality interactions. To improve your score, focus on adding substantive, topic-relevant insights, respond to others’ questions in detail, and aim for discussions that spark further dialogue. Participating in trending threads or answering new consultant questions quickly can also boost visibility and interaction, which may positively impact the score.

### 评论 #7 (作者: LB76673, 时间: 9个月前)

The  **community score**  isn’t only about how often you post or attend — it also measures  **quality, consistency, and engagement from others** . Daily comments alone can sometimes work against you if they’re seen as low-value or repetitive, since the scoring system rewards contributions that generate meaningful discussion, upvotes, or are tagged as helpful by peers. Similarly, participation in webinars or sessions helps, but the score seems to decay over time, so you need a steady stream of impactful activity to maintain it. To raise your score, focus on writing  **thoughtful, specific posts that show clear insights or practical experiences** , and try to answer questions from newer members in a way that’s actionable. It also helps to engage in follow-ups, not just drop a comment and leave. In short, it’s less about volume and more about sustained, valuable contributions that the community interacts with.
