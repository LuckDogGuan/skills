# Short-Lived Alphas vs. Sustainable Ones

## 帖子正文

In my recent experiments, I saw some alphas perform really well for a few months and then fade quickly. Others show smaller but consistent returns.
Do you prefer to chase these short-lived, high-sharpe opportunities, or focus only on signals that demonstrate longer-term durability?

---

## 讨论与评论 (4)

### 评论 #1 (作者: 顾问 JN96079 (Rank 44), 时间: 9个月前)

Longer-term returns are better. In fact, signals should try as much to show a consistent Shrape or performance, and this way, they showcase their chances of continuous best output in terms of returns. So, in short,  **I wouldn’t “only” focus on long-durability signals** , because then you risk missing a lot of alpha that is ephemeral but real. But  **I wouldn’t chase only the short-lived high Sharpe ones** , either, because of the risk, cost, and decay. The hybrid approach gives you flexibility and balance .^^JN
