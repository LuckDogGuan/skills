### 评论 #2 (作者: AJ93287, 时间: 2年前)

Denote the ferry that starts from the left-hand side, 'One' and the ferry that starts from the right-hand side 'Two'.

Two must travel at a speed 25% greater than One.

For example, consider the case where One travels at 10 metres-per-minute, which implies Two travels at 12.5 metres-per-minute.

By the time One has moved 400 metres, Two has moved 400 x 1.25 = 500 metres from the right-hand side, suggesting the river has a width of 400 + 500 = 900 metres.

 **Check:** One moves the remaining 500 metres, turns round and moves a further 300 metres to finish 300 metres from the right-hand side, giving a total further distance of 500 + 300 = 800 metres.
Two moves 800 x 1.25 = 1000 metres in the same time, which consists of travelling 400 metres, turning round and travelling another 600 metres: leaving it 300 metres from the right-hand side.

 **Final Answer:**  The river is 900 metres wide.

### 评论 #5 (作者: TN67143, 时间: 2年前)

First, Let suppose the width of the river is x. 

Let's assume the position of the left side of the river is (0,0), and the position of the right side of the river is (0, x).

Then, suppose the velocity of the ferry starting from the left side of the river is v1, and the velocity of the ferry starting from the right side is v2.

Next, we shall look at the first data event. They first met at point which is 400m from the left side. Let's suppose the time needed is t1. Then we have: the following equations:
1. v1*t1 + v2*t1 = x
2. v1*t1 = 400. (ferry from the left)
3. v2*t1 = x-400 (ferry from the right)

Then, they reach the other side and turnaround, before meeting again at the point located 300m from the right side. Let's suppose the time between first meeting and second meeting is t2. Then we have the following equations:
4. v1*t2 + v2*t2 = 2*x (since the total length that both ferry go from the first meeting and second meeting is 2 times the width of the river: they both reach the other side (x) before meeting again (x))
5. v1*t2 = (x-400)+300 = x-100 (ferry starting from the left)
6. v2*t2= 400+(x-300) = x+100 (ferry starting from the right.

Combining equation (1) and (4), we have: t2*(v1+v2) = 2*x = 2*t1*(v1+v2). Therefore, we have t2 = 2*t1.

From (5), we have v1*t2 = x-100 = v1*(2*t1) = 2*v1*t1 = 2*400 = 800. So, we have x = 900 (x-100 = 800).

So the final answer is 900m.

### 评论 #6 (作者: GD59214, 时间: 2年前)

The width of the river can be determined by following these steps:

1. Two ferries start from opposite sides of the river.
2. They first meet 400 meters from the left side.
3. They continue to the opposite side, turn around, and meet again 300 meters from the right side.

Let W be the width of the river.

Step-by-Step Solution:

First Meeting:
The left ferry travels 400 meters.
The right ferry travels W-400 meters.
Their speed ratio: 400/(W+400)

2. Second Meeting:

The left ferry travels W+300 meters.
The right ferry travels 2W-300 meters.
 Their speed ratio: W + 300/2W - 300.

Since the speed ratio is the same, we equate them:
400/W-400 =W + 300/2W - 300

Simplifying:

800W - 120000 = W^2 - 100W - 120000
W^2 - 900W = 0
W(W - 900) = 0

Therefore the width is 900 m.

Multiplying the (*) equation by v, we get (v/u)400+(v/u)(x-300)=x-100 ----(***). Again, multiplying the (**) equation by v, (v/u)400=x-400 -----(****).

Solving equations (***) and (****) for x, we get
x=700 meters.
