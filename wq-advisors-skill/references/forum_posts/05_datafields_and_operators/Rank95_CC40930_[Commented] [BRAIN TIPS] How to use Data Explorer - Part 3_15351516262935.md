**WorldQuant defines alphas as mathematical models that seek to predict the future price movements of various financial instruments.*

It’s often the case that you become familiar with some ideas and related datasets that you know are working reasonably well and tend to stick with them but more often than not, in order to build value-adding alphas, you have to step out of your comfort zone. Our Data Explorer tool allows you to browse existing datasets by categories or by using keywords. You can also apply filters to narrow down the scope of your searches.

When you find a dataset that aligns with your alpha research strategy, you can start studying the dataset and look for alpha ideas. Our  [Research Paper](/hc/en-us/community/topics/12997688420247-Research-Papers-for-Consultants)  series is a great place for you to find ideas on a variety of different topics.

When you are ready to get started with some new ideas, use Data Explorer to look for relevant data fields, and enter your search strings into the tool. To maximize your chances of getting the targeted fields – apply the following tactic: first, search for  **terminology** , then  **abbreviation**  and lastly,  **your own explanation**  of the concept. Similarly, you can apply filters within your searches.

Occasionally, you may find yourself in a situation where the data field you’re looking for doesn’t seem to appear even after many search attempts. For situations like this, you should find a proxy for the targeted data field and look it up instead. For example, “news count” can be used as a proxy for “attention towards a stock”. For complex concepts and formulas, you can also try breaking them down into smaller ideas - and then look for their component parts instead.

When you’re satisfied with the results, start building your alphas by clicking on the  **Simulate Field**  button. As you improve your alpha ideas, you can always look for more data fields.
