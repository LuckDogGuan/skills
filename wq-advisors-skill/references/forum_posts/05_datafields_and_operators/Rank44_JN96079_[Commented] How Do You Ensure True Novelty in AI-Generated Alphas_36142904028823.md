## 帖子正文

With the increasing use of LLMs in alpha generation, many signals may converge toward similar logic or operator patterns. How do you ensure that the AI-generated child alphas genuinely introduce  **novel structures or perspectives** , rather than slightly modified versions of existing logic? Do you use correlation filters, data diversity, or post-generation transformation to maintain uniqueness?

### 评论 #4 (作者: TP18957, 时间: 5个月前)

Ensuring true novelty in AI-generated alphas has become one of the core challenges as LLM usage scales, and I think the key is treating AI output as a starting point rather than a finished product. One effective approach is to deliberately constrain or rotate the search space: forcing generation across different dataset families, regions, and horizons helps reduce convergence toward the same operator templates. Beyond that, systematic correlation checks are essential—not only production correlation, but also IC correlation and exposure similarity, which often reveal hidden redundancy even when formulas look different. I’ve also found value in post-generation transformations, such as re-expressing signals with alternative normalizations, changing temporal aggregation, or decomposing a generated alpha into sub-components and recombining them differently. Finally, human intuition still matters: asking  *what economic behavior this signal represents*  often exposes whether it’s genuinely new or just a syntactic variation of an existing idea. Novelty emerges when structural behavior changes, not just when expressions look different.
