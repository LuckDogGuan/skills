# [BRAIN TIPS] Seven Tips for Creating Delay-0 (D0) Alphas

## 帖子正文

Delay is an option in the alpha settings that determines when your strategy takes positions in stocks. Delay=1 alphas trade in the morning using data from yesterday; delay=0 alphas trade in the afternoon using data from today.

Here are some tips to begin creating D0 alphas:

1. **Start with your D1 alpha ideas but use D0 data like price–volume** ; “ **open price** ,” for example, is a good source for potential signals.  In addition to price reversion ideas, try the  **group reversion concept** — i.e., you could calculate the mean value of the group and expect the value of individual stocks to revert to the mean of the group.

1. Next move to  **news D0 datasets**  where you could use the number of news items to be a trigger for event alphas. You could select rare events such as mergers or acquisitions (this data is only accessible to consultants), then assign value based on that or on the returns for the day the event occurs. You could also try betting on the saying  **“no news is good news”**  by creating alphas that look for stocks that no one mentions.

1. **D0 sentiment datasets**  often are derived from news or social media data and therefore share the same characteristics (liquid, low coverage, etc.). Since sentiment scores are the derived value, normally you could use them to try to create an alpha directly. You could also test reversion and technical indicators such as relative strength index (RSI) on sentiment data fields, instead of on price data field.

1. **Specific ideas** for D0 alphas are typically more effective when working with l **ow turnover datasets such as fundamental and analyst data** .

1. You can use some D1 data in D0 alphas — for example, group and relationship datasets.

1. D0 alphas often do well in  **liquid (smaller) universes** .

1. **Some alphas can work in both D0 and D1.**  There may be other alphas where the D0 version outperforms its D1 version. However, there can also be specific D0 alphas that simply behave differently in delay 0 compared to delay 1. For example, in D0, a stock’s price can rise and show a momentum trend. However in D1, due to selling, the stock can begin to show a reversion trend.
